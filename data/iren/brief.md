## Key Commitments

### Signed
- **Poolside AI GPU deployment** (Q2 FY2024): Named counterparty, 248 GPUs deployed, operational mechanics disclosed. No contract value or duration disclosed.
- **Bitmain option structure** (Q2 FY2024): 9 EH call option at $14/TH, contracted when Bitcoin was ~$30,000. Specific and verifiable.
- **T21→S21 Pro amendment** (Q3 FY2024): Option exercised and amended without deployment timeline impact — prior commitment revisited and confirmed.
- **Microsoft contract** (Q1 FY2026): $9.7 billion total, $1.94 billion annual revenue, 5-year term, $1.9 billion customer prepayments, $5.8 billion GPU CapEx, 85% project EBITDA margin, unlevered IRR "low double digits," levered IRR 25–30%.
- **$3.6 billion delayed draw term loan** (Q2 FY2026): Counterparties Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan, interest rate ceiling sub-6%, amortizing, asset-secured against GPUs and contracted Microsoft cash flows, covering ~95% of GPU-related CapEx.
- **2.3 GW of signed interconnection agreements** (Q1 FY2025 onward): Repeatedly distinguished from pipeline megawatts; cited as contractually secured.
- **~$2.3 billion ARR under contract** (Q2 FY2026): Microsoft anchor plus Prince George capacity, though the ~$0.4 billion non-Microsoft portion is partially "under negotiation."

### Aspirational
- **30 EH → 50 EH → beyond** (Q3 FY2024–Q3 FY2025): Successive exahash targets, delivered on early targets but the mining expansion is now capped at 50 EH by management decision.
- **Morgan Stanley West Texas process** (Q4 FY2024–Q2 FY2025): "NDAs, data room access, active conversations with some of the largest companies in the world." Morgan Stanley subsequently terminated; no deal announced.
- **Sweetwater 1.4 GW site monetization** (Q4 FY2024–Q2 FY2026, recurring): Described every quarter as "active conversations," "advanced stages," "strong levels of interest." No named counterparty, no signed term sheet disclosed across six consecutive quarters. April 2026 energization stated as on track.
- **Horizon 1 liquid-cooled facility** (Q1–Q2 FY2025): "Highly confident of contracting ahead of commissioning." No anchor tenant named as of Q2 FY2026.
- **40,000 additional GPUs / ~$1 billion ARR** (Q1 FY2026–Q2 FY2026): GPUs not yet purchased at Q1 FY2026 announcement; no named customers disclosed at Q2 FY2026. Represents ~30% of the $3.4 billion ARR target.
- **$3.4 billion ARR by end of calendar 2026** (Q1–Q2 FY2026): Presented with confidence co-equal to the signed Microsoft agreement; the non-Microsoft ~$1 billion component has no signed contracts behind it.
- **"Potential investor distributions in 2025"** (Q1 FY2025): Subsequently deferred in Q2 FY2025 with explicit acknowledgment; no mechanism announced as of Q2 FY2026.

## FOG Patterns

**Recurring phrases across nine quarters (degrading, not improving):**

1. **"We're excited / super excited / incredibly excited"** — Present in every quarter from Q3 FY2023 through Q2 FY2026. By Q3 FY2025, the analyst notes it appears in the same breath as unsubstantiated demand claims. Frequency has not declined.

2. **"Very strong / extremely strong demand"** — The Q1 FY2026 analysis flags this appearing "at least four times across speakers" with zero quantification. Q2 FY2026 repeats: "demand is not the constraint for us." This phrase is unfalsifiable by design — it cannot be contradicted by any observable fact short of signed contracts, which are withheld.

3. **"Uniquely positioned"** — Flagged in Q3 FY2025 appearing four times without substantiation. Reappears in Q4 FY2025 and Q2 FY2026. No competitive benchmark is ever offered.

4. **"Active conversations / advanced stages of negotiation / robust pipeline"** — Applied to Sweetwater for six consecutive quarters without converting to a named counterparty. The phrase "strong levels of interest" (Q1 FY2026) and "multiple advanced negotiations underway" (Q2 FY2026) are structurally identical to language used in Q4 FY2024.

5. **"It does feel like / we believe / we fundamentally believe"** — Epistemic hedges attached to very large market projections ($5T, 346M to 950M AI users) that create an impression of evidence-based conviction without actual evidence.

**Verdict:** Linguistic precision has not improved over nine quarters. The FOG is structural, not situational — it appears in prepared remarks regardless of whether the quarter brought good or bad news. The single improvement was FOG reduction in Q1 FY2026 (grade B- vs. prior C), attributable entirely to the Microsoft contract forcing specific language. Q2 FY2026 reverted to C. The Q&A has consistently been cleaner than prepared remarks throughout the history, suggesting Roberts is capable of precision but defaults to promotion when scripted.

## Flags & Risks

**Data Quality Flags:**
- Total equity declined from $2,876.2M (Q3 FY2025) to $2,511.2M (Q4 FY2025) — a $365M single-quarter reduction that is not explained in available narrative. Warrants verification.
- Q2 FY2026 adjusted EBITDA not quantified in prepared remarks — a regression from prior disclosure practice.
- Transcription error in Q1 FY2026: "$1 million in 0 coupon convertible notes" almost certainly means $1 billion; not corrected on the call.
- Convertible note maturity date in Q2 FY2025 stated as "June 15, 2023" — a past date, likely a transcription error not caught or corrected.
- FCF of -$654.7M in Q1 FY2025 on $144.8M revenue reflects CapEx intensity of the buildout but is not contextualized against any funding framework disclosed that quarter.

**Communication-Level Risks (aggregate):**
- **Perishable goal replacement**: Sweetwater has been described as imminent for six quarters. Each quarter introduces new milestones (Morgan Stanley process terminated, Sweetwater 2 announced, April 2026 energization) that reset the aspirational clock without acknowledging the prior gap.
- **AI pipeline conflation**: The $3.4 billion ARR target includes ~$1 billion with no signed contracts. This is presented with confidence co-equal to the $2.3 billion in signed/near-signed agreements.
- **Dilution undisclosed as cost**: $823M raised via ATM through FY2024; $9.2B in total capital raises disclosed by Q2 FY2026. The cost-of-capital framework for dilutive issuance has never been disclosed, despite Roberts's stated aversion to dilution in AI contexts.
- **Zero problem disclosure** across nine quarters: The Horizon 1 rack density miscalibration (Q4 FY2025) is the sole disclosed execution error, framed as strategic agility. No cost overruns, customer losses, or construction delays acknowledged in any quarter.
- **Co-CEO Will Roberts entirely absent** from Q2 FY2026 call despite being cited as a governance strength.
- **Customer concentration**: AI Cloud revenue was effectively 100% Poolside through Q3 FY2024; Poolside subsequently departed. No customer concentration metrics disclosed at any point.
