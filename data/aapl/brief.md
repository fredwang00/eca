**Direction: Stagnating.** The composite has oscillated between 2.25 and 2.635 over ten quarters without a durable directional shift. No quarter has scored above a B or below a C. The inflection point that briefly elevated scores was Q2 FY2024 (2.55, B), driven by Dimension 3 (Stakeholder Balance) rising to a B on the strength of named enterprise examples and quantified environmental claims — but this did not hold. Q2 FY2025 reached the series high (2.635) primarily on stronger Strategic Clarity grading as Apple disclosed the C1 modem internalization and offered a conditional tariff estimate — then retreated. The defining structural feature across all ten quarters is the same: Dimension 1 (Capital Stewardship) holds at B throughout, while Dimensions 2 (Strategic Clarity) and 4 (FOG Index) persistently grade at C, capping the composite. The Maestri-to-Parekh CFO transition shows no change in the financial candor standard. Cook's communication style has not materially evolved across the full period.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality flags:**
- Q3 FY2025 total revenue and iPhone revenue are absent from the structured financial data provided; gross margin, EPS, operating cash flow, and capital return figures are present.
- Q4 FY2023 net cash not recorded in structured data.
- Q3 FY2024 dividends not recorded in structured data.
- Q1 FY2025 EPS not recorded in structured data.
- Q4 FY2025 "adjusted" EPS of $1.85 (up 13% YoY) excludes an unnamed, unsized Q4 FY2024 onetime charge — disclosed only in passing, never explained in either prepared remarks or Q&A.
- Services gross margin declined 60bps sequentially in Q3 FY2024 with no causal explanation offered; this remains unreconciled.

**Communication-level risks (aggregated across the series):**

1. **Google TAC exposure is structurally undisclosed.** Estimated at $18–20B annually by analysts, the arrangement is embedded in guidance assumptions ("assumes the current revenue share agreement with Google continues") without being sized or explained. Active DOJ litigation as of Q3 FY2023 has continued through Q1 FY2026. Cook declined to address contingency planning in Q3 FY2025 and Q1 FY2026. This is the most financially material undisclosed item in the series.

2. **Personalized Siri is a compounding perishable goal.** "Coming this year" or "next year" has appeared across Q4 FY2023, Q1 FY2024, Q3 FY2024, Q1 FY2025, Q2 FY2025, Q3 FY2025, Q4 FY2025, and Q1 FY2026 without a single narrowing of specification, milestone, or accountability structure. Eight consecutive quarters of forward commitment without falsifiable content.

3. **AI demand causality is asserted, not evidenced.** The claim that Apple Intelligence drove iPhone 16 outperformance is repeated across Q1 FY2025, Q4 FY2025, and Q1 FY2026 without any quantitative engagement metrics, upgrade rate differentials by AI-availability region, or controlled comparison. The same phrase ("becoming a greater factor") has been used for three consecutive quarters without progression.

4. **$500B / $600B domestic investment is politically framed, not investor-accountable.** Announced Q1 FY2025, revised upward to $600B by Q4 FY2025, cited in Q1 FY2026 — no CapEx/OpEx breakdown, no timeline milestones, no prior-period accountability structure. Cook's response to direct composition questions: "Lots of all of it."

5. **China candor gap persists.** From Q1 FY2024 onward, Cook consistently maintains long-term optimism while providing inadequate structural analysis of Huawei's re-emergence and domestic competition dynamics. Q2 FY2025 produced the notable non-bridge: "I can't bridge to numbers we didn't come up with." The Q4 FY2025 prediction that China would return to growth in Q1 FY2026 is the first falsifiable China commitment in the series.

6. **Prior-quarter guidance reconciliation is structurally absent.** Across all ten quarters, no call opens with an explicit accounting of what was guided last quarter and whether it was met. Investors must perform this reconstruction from external sources. This is a persistent, structural accountability gap.

7. **Google AI partnership terms undisclosed.** Announced Q1 FY2026 — no commercial terms, no exclusivity disclosure, no relationship to the OpenAI arrangement addressed, no privacy-brand implication analyzed. Cook declined to characterize the arrangement beyond "collaboration."

## Verify Next Quarter

At the Q2 FY2026 call (expected April 2026), verify the following against the Q1 FY2026 commitments:

1. **Supply constraint resolution.** Cook identified three-nanometer node availability as the specific bottleneck and described Apple as in "supply chase mode" with "less flexibility in the supply chain than normal." The March quarter guidance range of 13–16% revenue growth "comprehends constrained iPhone supply." Was supply constrained throughout the quarter? Was the revenue shortfall quantified retroactively? Did supply-demand balance emerge, and if so, when?

2. **Memory inflation impact in Q2.** Parekh confirmed memory pricing would be "a bit more of an impact to Q2 gross margin" and Cook acknowledged prices "increasing significantly" beyond Q2. The next call must disclose the actual memory headwind and whether any of the unspecified "range of options" (LTAs, spot purchases, consumer price adjustments) was executed.

3. **Personalized Siri progress.** Cook committed to "a more personalized Siri coming this year" — now the ninth consecutive quarter of a forward commitment without falsifiable content. The April call should be evaluated for: (a) any WWDC 2026 announcement, (b) any narrowing of feature definition or rollout geography, or (c) any causal explanation of what specifically caused prior delays. If the language is again repeated verbatim, log as a confirmed perishable goal across nine quarters.

4. **China growth return.** Cook stated explicitly in Q4 FY2025: "We do believe that we'll return to growth in Q1." Q1 FY2026 reported Greater China results must be checked against this specific, falsifiable prediction.

5. **App Store sub-category growth reconciliation.** Parekh declined to address third-party data showing App Store growth of ~7% against the 14% Services headline, citing a policy against sub-category disclosure. If Services growth continues to diverge from app economy benchmarks, the next call should be evaluated for whether any category-level granularity is voluntarily introduced or whether the policy of non-disclosure on this specific line is maintained.

6. **Google partnership disclosure progression.** Cook declined to disclose any commercial terms. Check the Q2 FY2026 call and any intervening 10-Q, 8-K, or Google earnings disclosures for revenue share structure, exclusivity terms, minimum guarantees, or any characterization of the arrangement analogous to the Google Search default deal.

7. **Tariff cost of ~$1.4B for December quarter.** This estimate was provided with the same methodology as prior quarters. Verify whether the actual figure is disclosed at Q2 with equivalent precision, and whether the March quarter estimate is provided with the same conditional framing and non-extrapolation warning.
