**Direction: Oscillating, not compounding.** The grade traces a sawtooth pattern between B (2.80) and A (3.65) rather than trending in either direction. Two named inflection points:

- **Q3 FY2024 (November 2023)** — First A-grade. Marked by front-loaded voluntary disclosure of expense misses, proactive LIFO and hurricane charge quantification, and McMillon's real-time correction of analyst optimism on alternative revenue. Sets the ceiling.
- **Q4 FY2026 (February 2026)** — First call under new CEO John Furner. Score reverts to 2.80, driven by FOG grade of C, platitude-heavy prepared remarks ("agentic commerce will be great"), absence of any "what went wrong" disclosure, and three new segment leaders providing uniformly enthusiastic, content-free introductions. Represents a measurable communication regression at CEO transition.

The CFO (Rainey) sustains the floor across all quarters. The CEO (McMillon, then Furner) drives variance. McMillon's calls correlate with the A-range quarters; Furner's Q4 debut pushed the composite to the B floor.

## FOG Patterns

Recurring vague language identified across all nine quarters. Precision has **not improved** over time; in Q4 FY2026 (Furner's debut), FOG density measurably increased.

**Persistent phrases, quarter-by-quarter recurrence:**

1. **"We like who we are / we like what we're seeing / we feel good about"** — Appears in Q1 FY2024, Q1 FY2025, Q2 FY2025, Q3 FY2025, Q4 FY2026. Pure sentiment substituting for mechanism. Most concentrated in CEO prepared remarks.

2. **"Positioned for growth" / "strong momentum" / "good momentum"** — Q2 FY2024 (twice), Q2 FY2025 (four instances), Q4 FY2026 (four instances). "Momentum" is never defined against a benchmark; "positioned" is never falsified.

3. **"Our value proposition continues to resonate"** — Q1 FY2024, Q3 FY2024, Q2 FY2025, Q4 FY2026. "Resonate" consistently appears without a behavioral metric (repeat purchase rate, NPS, penetration) to substantiate it.

4. **"People-led, tech-powered"** — McMillon's branded slogan appears in Q2 FY2024 and Q1 FY2025, presented as though it constitutes explanation. Unchanged in cadence or substance across all uses.

5. **"We're excited about [initiative]"** — AI, advertising, Sparky, generative AI, pharmacy, remodels — applied indiscriminately to every initiative mentioned, removing signal value from enthusiasm entirely. Most concentrated in Q2 FY2024 and Q4 FY2026.

6. **"Agentic commerce will be great"** (Q4 FY2026) — Introduced with zero definition, no metrics, no timeline. The most acute single-quarter FOG spike in the series.

**Counter-evidence of precision (demonstrating the capability exists):** Rainey's RIM/LIFO proactive disclosure (Q1 FY2026), the explicit 200bp e-commerce contribution goal accountability (Q4 FY2024), and the $450M claims expense breakdown (Q2 FY2026) show the organization is capable of precision when it chooses it. FOG is selective, not structural — concentrated in CEO prepared remarks and strategic framing, sparse in CFO financial commentary.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality flags:**
- ROI calculation basis conflates organic improvement with mechanical lapping of one-time charges (opioid settlement + Flipkart/PhonePe, $4.2B) for at least four quarters. Rainey disclosed this proactively but the distinction matters for trend interpretation.
- E-commerce profitability reported as percentage improvement from undisclosed base through Q3 FY2025 ("40% loss reduction"), then as directional ("losses narrowing"), then confirmed profitable in Q1 FY2026 — but absolute margin level never disclosed. "Roughly double-digit incremental margins" (Q4 FY2026) is the only bound.
- Advertising growth in Q2 FY2026 reported as +50% without the Vizio adjustment until analyst Q&A; apples-to-apples was +27%. The headline number appears first in prepared remarks.
- International segment across all quarters: margin and return-on-investment figures consistently absent. McLay's contributions are market-by-market revenue metrics only.

**Communication-level risks (aggregated):**

- **Perishable goals pattern**: Specific targets introduced in one quarter (200bp e-commerce contribution, 900 remodels, 100% Computer Vision deployment, Sam's Club openings) are rarely revisited with explicit close-out. Prior commitments are replaced with new ones without stated disposition.
- **Guidance sandbagging**: Q1 FY2025 EPS guided $0.49–$0.52; delivered $0.60 (+15–22% beat). Pattern of large beats with guidance not raised in response. If sustained, guidance ceases to carry information.
- **CEO transition communication regression**: Q4 FY2026 FOG score (C) is the lowest in the series. Furner's prepared remarks and Q&A exhibit the platitude patterns that McMillon had partially contained. Three new segment leaders produced uniformly positive, content-free introductions.
- **Tariff opacity**: Q4 FY2025 and Q1 FY2026 both deflected tariff quantification. "We don't have any explicit assumption in our guidance" (Rainey, Q4 FY2025) is non-disclosure dressed as process confidence.
- **Supplier tension named but unresolved**: McMillon's Q1 FY2024 criticism of branded suppliers in dry grocery ("we do need some of these branded suppliers to get top line focused") signaled a negotiating dynamic Walmart does not control. Not revisited explicitly in subsequent quarters.

## Verify Next Quarter

From Q4 FY2026, the following commitments are on record and should be checked on the Q1 FY2027 call:

1. **Sparky metrics stability or evolution.** Q4 FY2026 baselines: ~50% of app users engaged; ~35% higher AOV vs. non-users. Check whether these figures are restated as baselines with updated progress, or silently replaced by new metrics — metric substitution = perishable goal confirmed.

2. **"Agentic commerce" definition and milestone.** No quantitative threshold was offered in Q4 FY2026. If the term appears again without a measurable adoption rate, revenue contribution, or basket attribution threshold, flag as confirmed perishable goal.

3. **January reorganization early evidence.** Furner described Seth's enterprise role (marketplace, Vizio, advertising, Walmart+) as enabling "build once, scale globally." Check: (a) any named international market where a US capability was deployed from shared infrastructure, (b) any cost or time-to-market metric.

4. **Advertising growth rate trajectory.** Q4 FY2026: Walmart Connect U.S. +41%; global $6.4B (+37%). Expected moderation to 25–30% range given base growth. A sharper deceleration would indicate Vizio integration friction or competitive pressure worth pressing.

5. **MFP pharmacy pricing headwind quantification.** Rainey cited ~100bps full-year and ~30bps Q4 partial impact. Q1 FY2027 will show the first full-quarter effect of the January implementation. Watch for whether management provides a specific comp basis point impact or generalizes it into "category mix pressure."

6. **Furner's candor baseline establishment.** Q4 FY2026 is one call. The critical test is whether Q1 FY2027 shows improvement in specificity (closing out prior commitments, naming what went wrong, offering falsifiable milestones) or whether the FOG patterns consolidate into a new communications norm under new leadership.
