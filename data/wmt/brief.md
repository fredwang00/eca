The pattern across all twelve quarters is a consistent two-tier structure—CFO communication scoring A-level, CEO/segment communication scoring B or below—with the composite pulled toward B by persistent FOG in prepared remarks.

## Key Commitments

**Signed:**
- Advertising revenue: $4.4B globally (FY2025, +27%), $6.4B globally (FY2026, +37%). Named business: Walmart Connect U.S.
- Membership income: $3.8B (FY2025, +21%), exceeding $4.3B (FY2026, +21%). Named business: Sam's Club, Walmart+.
- WFS penetration: record ~50% (FY2025, +600bps); 52% of sellers using WFS (Q4 FY2026).
- Share repurchases: $6B+ year-to-date through Q2 FY2026; $30B new authorization disclosed Q4 FY2026.
- CapEx: $23.8B (FY2025); guided ~3.5% of sales (FY2026).
- Operating cash flow: $42B (FY2026); $27B year-to-date through Q3 FY2026.
- Dividend increase: 13% (FY2026), described as "largest in over a decade."
- E-commerce losses: reduced 80% year-over-year (FY2025); U.S., Sam's Club, and enterprise-level profitable in Q1 FY2026.
- Automation: 50%+ of fulfillment center volume automated (Q3 FY2025); 9 automated RDCs operational, 6 under construction (Q3 FY2024).
- Sam's Club associate turnover: down 1,700 basis points year-over-year following wage investment (Q4 FY2025).
- VIZIO acquisition: disclosed as "slightly dilutive to EPS near term," financing via cash and/or debt (Q4 FY2024). Deal closed within FY2025.
- PhonePe: $700M noncash charge (Q3 FY2026); IPO described as "early steps" (Q4 FY2025).

**Aspirational:**
- "Agentic commerce" / "AgenTek commerce": introduced Q4 FY2026 across six or more mentions with no definition, adoption metric, or timeline.
- Sparky AI agent: 35% higher AOV for users, "half our app users" engaged — no baseline, no conversion rate, no milestone.
- OpenAI and Alphabet partnerships: named counterparties, but no contract terms, deployment scope, or financial commitment disclosed.
- "Build once, scale globally" platform thesis: stated three times in Q4 FY2026 without a single supporting metric.
- International e-commerce profitability: described as "a loss" contribution at enterprise level through Q2 FY2026; no timeline committed.
- 95% U.S. population with sub-three-hour delivery: framed as "we'll soon reach" (Q1 FY2026), with 93% cited. Not yet confirmed.
- General merchandise recovery: attributed to comparison-period easing, not demand-side evidence, across multiple quarters.
- Tariff navigation: "we feel like we'll be able to navigate that" (Q4 FY2025, Q1 FY2026) — no mechanism, no exposure quantification despite disclosure of China, Mexico, Vietnam, India, Canada as major sourcing markets.

## FOG Patterns

**Recurring phrases across quarters, cited verbatim or near-verbatim:**

1. **"We like who we are and where we're going"** — Q1 FY2024, Q1 FY2025. Sentiment assertion, zero information content. Present in McMillon's prepared remarks and adopted by Rainey.
2. **"Positioned for growth" / "strong momentum"** — Q2 FY2024 (twice), Q2 FY2025, Q4 FY2026. Used without benchmark, baseline, or attribution.
3. **"Resonating with customers"** — Q1 FY2024, Q3 FY2024, Q4 FY2025, Q1 FY2026. Appears as sentence opener before supporting data, making it structurally redundant.
4. **"Laser focused on the member and customer"** — Q1 FY2025 (twice). Corporate filler.
5. **"People-led, tech-powered"** — Q2 FY2024, Q1 FY2025, Q2 FY2025. An internal slogan presented as though it constitutes explanation.
6. **"We feel good about / we're excited about / we love what we're seeing"** — Dominant in Q2 FY2025 and Q4 FY2026 Q&A. Substitutes sentiment for substance when analysts probe for mechanism or quantification.
7. **"We're just getting started"** — Q4 FY2025 (used by both McMillon and Rainey in prepared remarks, signaling coordinated messaging rather than independent conviction).
8. **AI genericism** — "seizing the moment with AI" (Q2 FY2026); "it will be great for customers and associates" (Q4 FY2026); each quarter's AI commentary could be transplanted to any technology-adjacent earnings call without modification.

**Trend: Degrading.** The FOG index was B across FY2024 with episodic lapses. It stabilized at B through FY2025, driven by Rainey's numerical discipline. Q4 FY2026 produced the call's first C-grade on FOG under the new CEO, with "agentic commerce" introduced as a multi-instance buzzword without definition and "good momentum" appearing four times across speakers. The Q&A FOG pattern — where McMillon would occasionally deflect to talking points — appears to be consolidating under Furner into a more consistent habit.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality flags:**
- Non-GAAP ("constant currency") framing is used throughout without verbal reconciliation to reported results in prepared remarks across all twelve quarters. Investors must consult supplemental materials.
- E-commerce absolute loss levels were never disclosed. "80% improvement" (FY2025) and "roughly double-digit incremental margins" (Q4 FY2026) are percentages without denominators. Rainey explicitly declined to quantify magnitude when directly asked (Q1 FY2026).
- Prior-period guidance is revisited inconsistently. Q1 FY2026 is the strongest example of explicit accountability; Q4 FY2026 and Q2 FY2026 confirm outperformance directionally but do not walk "guided X, delivered Y" with full arithmetic.
- Walmart+ member counts are never disclosed in absolute terms across twelve quarters. Only growth rates or relative comparisons are offered.

**Communication-level risks:**
- **Perishable goals pattern.** New milestones (AI agents, 900 remodels, MFC deployment) enter the narrative without explicit closure of prior commitments. "Perishable" is the correct diagnosis — each quarter's language is internally coherent but not anchored to the prior quarter's specific promises.
- **Process reassurance as deflection.** "We have experienced merchants," "we have tools," "our team is managing that daily" — used in Q1 FY2026 and Q4 FY2026 when operational specificity was unavailable or uncomfortable. This is a structurally dishonest substitute for "we genuinely don't know yet."
- **The tariff non-disclosure is a live accountability exposure.** Both McMillon and Rainey declined to model tariff assumptions through Q1 FY2026, with explicit guidance conditioned on tariff levels remaining stable. If tariff impacts materialize materially, every prior "we'll be able to navigate that" statement becomes a credibility marker missed.
- **Claims expense categorization.** $730M in Q1–Q2 FY2026, partially described as industry-wide. No analysis of whether Walmart's operational practices (delivery pace, automation pace) contributed to claim frequency or severity. Characterizing a recurring cost as unusual without this analysis is a mild but trackable transparency gap.
- **Leadership transition FOG risk.** Furner's Q4 FY2026 debut produced the lowest CEO-layer specificity in the series. If Q1 FY2027 produces another C-grade on FOG with no falsifiable AI milestones and omnichannel boilerplate, the communication quality trajectory under the new CEO will be confirmed as declining.

## Verify Next Quarter

*Carrying forward from Q4 FY2026 (February 2026) into Q1 FY2027:*

1. **Sparky adoption and AOV metrics.** Q4 FY2026 baseline: ~50% of app users engaged, 35% higher AOV vs. non-users. Verify whether these figures are restated, updated directionally, or quietly replaced by new metrics. Metric substitution without comparison to the prior baseline is the perishable goal signal.

2. **Agentic commerce — definition or milestone.** "Agentic commerce" / "AgenTek commerce" was introduced with no definition or measurable threshold. If the Q1 call references this term again without a quantitative anchor (adoption rate, revenue contribution, basket attribution), flag as confirmed promotional narrative.

3. **Platform reorganization early evidence.** The January enterprise reorganization (marketplace, VIZIO, advertising, Walmart+ under unified leadership) was stated to enable "build once, scale globally." Q1 should be examined for any named international market where a US-developed capability was deployed, or any cost/time-to-market metric from shared infrastructure. Absence = unconfirmed.

4. **Advertising growth rate trajectory.** Q4 FY2026 global advertising: $6.4B, +37%; Walmart Connect U.S. +41%. Rainey acknowledged the law-of-large-numbers challenge. Track whether growth moderates to the previously stated 25–30% range (consistent with maturation) or decelerates more sharply, signaling competitive pressure or VIZIO integration drag.

5. **Maximum fair pricing (MFP) health and wellness impact.** Rainey explicitly declined to quantify the January 2026 legislative impact in Q3 FY2026. Q1 FY2027 will include the first full quarter of MFP effect. Verify whether a specific basis-point estimate is provided, or whether the deflection pattern continues.

6. **Tariff impact specificity.** FY2026 guidance was explicitly conditioned on tariff stability. Q1 FY2027 should be evaluated for whether management provides specific tariff-related cost or margin quantification, or whether "we'll navigate this" remains the entirety of the disclosure.
