## FOG Patterns

**Recurring phrases identified across multiple quarters:**

1. **"Resonating with customers/members"** — appears Q1 FY2024, Q3 FY2024, Q2 FY2025, Q2 FY2026, Q1 FY2026. Never followed by a methodology, NPS score, or defined measurement.

2. **"We like who we are / we like our position / we like where we're going"** — Q1 FY2024 (McMillon), Q1 FY2025 (Rainey). Identity affirmation without evidence.

3. **"People-led, tech-powered"** — every quarter from Q2 FY2024 through Q4 FY2026. Branded slogan presented as explanation.

4. **"Positioned for growth" / "strong momentum"** — Q2 FY2024 (twice, no specificity); Q2 FY2025 (Rainey, "momentum" without benchmark); Q4 FY2026 (Furner/Gagina, "good momentum" four times).

5. **"Excited/encouraged about early reads/results"** — Q1 FY2024, Q2 FY2024, Q3 FY2025. When applied uniformly regardless of initiative maturity, eliminates signal value.

6. **"Laser focused on the customer/member"** — Q1 FY2025 (twice verbatim). Filler.

7. **"We feel like we're just getting started"** — Q4 FY2025, used by both McMillon and Rainey in prepared remarks, signaling a coached phrase.

8. **"[Technology] will be great for our customers and associates"** — AI iteration appears Q2 FY2025, Q3 FY2026, Q4 FY2026. Each instance lacks timeline, metric, or mechanism.

**Linguistic precision trend: degrading.** The Q3 FY2024 call represents the high-water mark for FOG avoidance—executives led with data and used sentiment as punctuation, not as substance. Since Q2 FY2025, FOG has become more structurally embedded: the Q2 FY2025 FOG grade was C, Q4 FY2026 returned to C under Furner's leadership. The Q1 FY2026 call (A overall) is the exception, where tariff specificity and RIM/LIFO disclosure temporarily displaced the pattern.

The most concerning development is the introduction of undefined category language—"agentic commerce," "AgenTek commerce"—in Q4 FY2026 without retirement of prior terminology. This is the textbook perishable goal structure: introduce a new label, attach impressive-sounding metrics without baselines, and reset the narrative clock.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality flags:**
- No equity decline, cash position deterioration, or GAAP/non-GAAP discontinuity was identified across the structured data. ROI declined in FY2024 due to identified prior-period charges and subsequently recovered to multi-year highs.
- Non-GAAP constant currency framing is used throughout without verbal GAAP reconciliation in prepared remarks. Listeners must consult supplemental materials.
- Q2 FY2026 gross margin may be artificially elevated by RIM markup accounting; Rainey telegraphed this but investors without access to the verbal caveat may misread the margin improvement.

**Communication-level risks (aggregated):**

- **Perishable goal pattern is systematic.** Prior targets are not formally closed: the FY2025 advertising/membership 20%-of-OI target was never reconciled against actuals; the FY2028 international doubling commitment has received no progress update; the "200bps e-commerce contribution profit improvement" from Q2 FY2024 was declared delivered but without a specific figure. New goals replace old ones without explicit acknowledgment across every quarter reviewed.

- **E-commerce profitability timeline is perpetually deferred.** Language has evolved from "on track" (FY2024) to "200bps improvement" (FY2024) to "40% loss reduction" (FY2024) to "a little further down the road" (FY2025) to "really close to a free cash flow inflection" (FY2026) to "we don't even talk about it internally—we've far surpassed breakeven" (Q4 FY2026). The progression reaches a positive resolution, but incremental margins ("roughly double-digit") remain the only disclosed figure. Absolute e-commerce loss magnitude was explicitly declined when requested (Q1 FY2026).

- **Claims expense may be secular, not episodic.** The $730M incremental claims expense in H1 FY2026 was attributed partly to an industry-wide trend. If claims remain elevated in H2 FY2026 and FY2027, management's "unusual" characterization from Q2 FY2026 will require revisiting.

- **Furner's first full call (Q4 FY2026) introduces fresh accountability gaps.** The January reorganization rationale ("build once, scale globally") carries no success metric, no timeline, and no named responsible executive for international replication. The new segment leaders' introductions were uniformly positive with no inherited-challenge disclosure.

- **Guidance conservatism is structurally significant.** Q1 FY2025 actual EPS beat guidance midpoint by 15–22%. Rainey explicitly declined to raise guidance despite the magnitude of the beat. When guidance beats are this large and consistent, and management declines to narrow ranges, guidance conveys reduced informational content.

## Verify Next Quarter

*From Q4 FY2026 tracking commitments and open threads:*

1. **Sparky metrics.** Q4 FY2026 establishes ~50% of app users engaged with Sparky; Sparky users show ~35% higher AOV. Q1 FY2027 should be checked: are these figures updated directionally, restated as baselines without change, or replaced by new metrics? Stasis or metric substitution confirms perishable goal pattern.

2. **"Agentic commerce" definition and milestone.** The term appeared six or more times in Q4 FY2026 without a definition, conversion rate, or revenue attribution. If Q1 FY2027 uses the term again without introducing a measurable threshold, flag as narrative without accountability.

3. **January reorganization early results.** Furner described moving marketplace/Vizio/advertising/Walmart+ to enterprise level under Seth. Q1 FY2027 should produce: (a) any international market where a US-developed capability was deployed, or (b) any cost or time-to-market improvement quantified. Absent either, the "build once, scale globally" thesis is unconfirmed.

4. **Advertising growth rate trajectory.** Q4 FY2026 reported global advertising at $6.4B, growing 37% (Walmart Connect U.S. +41%). Given law-of-large-numbers dynamics Rainey himself acknowledged, watch for whether growth decelerates to the 25–30% range (consistent with maturation) or falls more sharply, which would indicate competitive pressure or Vizio integration friction.

5. **Maximum fair pricing (MFP) impact on health and wellness.** Rainey declined to quantify the January impact in Q3 FY2026. Q4 FY2026 estimated ~100bps full-year headwind. Q1 FY2027 should show whether health and wellness comp trends confirm or contradict this estimate, and whether management provides a more specific timeline for when comps normalize.

6. **Claims expense trajectory.** Rainey stated in Q2 FY2026 that "we do not expect the same magnitude of increases" in H2. Q4 FY2026 guidance absorbed the known $730M H1 increment. Q1 FY2027 should be checked for whether claims expense returns toward plan or shows another step-up—either confirming episodic or secular characterization.

7. **Furner's communication discipline trajectory.** This is the first full Furner call as CEO. The FOG grade (C) and aspirational framing of agentic commerce represent the baseline. Track whether subsequent calls tighten toward Rainey's precision or whether platitude density increases as Furner's team settles into a communication pattern.
