**Direction: Stagnating with a single positive outlier and one sharp trough.**

Q3 2023 (2.80/B) is the only quarter that cleared the B threshold, driven by Musk's mechanistic explanation of FSD forecasting failures and the Cybertruck risk disclosure. The inflection downward hit Q1 2024 (1.55/D) — the lowest score in the series — triggered by an unacknowledged strategic pivot away from the unboxed manufacturing platform, Musk's refusal to answer Sacconaghi's direct question on vehicle strategy, and the highest FOG concentration across all quarters. Recovery from Q1 2024 was partial and flat. Q4 2025 (2.40) matches the Q2 2023 starting point — nine quarters of call evolution produced no net improvement in disclosure quality.

The FOG Index is the most consistently weak dimension: D or D+ in Q1 2024, Q4 2024, Q1 2025, and Q2 2025. It improved only in Q3 2025 (C) and Q4 2025 (C), driven by Taneja's financial specificity pulling up a Musk-dominated register. Capital Stewardship has been the most consistent strength, holding B across most quarters, entirely attributable to Taneja.

## Key Commitments

### Signed
No named counterparties, disclosed dollar values, or binding contract terms were announced across any of the nine quarters reviewed. The following items came closest but did not clear the bar:

- **IRA Manufacturing Credits:** $150M–$250M/quarter range confirmed Q2 2023, within guidance. Q3 2024 disclosed "over $2 billion of revenues so far this year" from regulatory credits — voluntarily disclosed, verifiable, but not a contract with a named counterparty.
- **FSD Transferability (Q3 2023):** Announced as a "onetime amnesty" for Q3 orders — specific, time-bound, and executed. No counterparty or dollar value.
- **FSD Licensing:** Musk stated Q1 2024 "I think we have a good chance we do sign a deal this year, maybe more than one." No deal was named across subsequent quarters. As of Q4 2025, no signed FSD license agreement has been disclosed.

### Aspirational
These claims lack counterparty names, dollar values, or binding terms:

- **Cybertruck volume production:** "Roughly 250,000/year by 2025" (Q3 2023); "high volume next year" (Q2 2023). No binding production commitment. Never explicitly reconciled against actuals.
- **Next-generation vehicle / cheaper model:** "Start of production planned for June [2025]" (Q1 2025); "ramp maybe might be a little slower" (Q1 2025). No price, margin target, or volume floor stated across nine quarters.
- **FSD "better than human" timelines:** Made in Q2 2023, Q3 2023, Q2 2024, Q3 2024, Q4 2024 — each time in the form "by end of this year" or "highly probable next year." No regulatory filing, safety submission, or third-party verification cited.
- **Austin robotaxi launch June 2025:** Committed Q4 2024, reaffirmed Q1 2025, Q2 2025. Described as starting with "maybe 10 or 20 vehicles on day one." Not a signed service agreement.
- **Optimus revenue north of $10 trillion:** Q4 2024. No addressable market logic, timeline, or competitive framework.
- **FSD licensing pipeline:** "A few major OEMs" (Q2 2024). No names, no terms, no timeline. Reclassified as promotional pipeline language given zero disclosed progress through Q4 2025.
- **CyberCab production start April 2026:** Q4 2025. Specific month, no volume commitment or financing structure disclosed.
- **Six new factories + TerraFab + solar fab + AI5/AI6:** Q4 2025. Named but unsequenced, unfinanced, and unanchored to delivery milestones.
- **"Quarter to half of the U.S." autonomous coverage by end 2026:** Q4 2025. Geographic claim conditioned on regulatory approvals with no defined trigger.

## FOG Patterns

### Recurring phrases across multiple quarters (verbatim or near-verbatim):

- **"Demand is off the hook" / "quasi-infinite demand":** Q2 2023 (Cybertruck), Q1 2024 (robotaxi), Q4 2024 (Optimus). Unsupported demand assertion deployed whenever a quantitative demand figure is owed.
- **"The most valuable company in the world by far":** Q4 2023, Q4 2024, Q1 2025 (×3), Q2 2025 (×3), Q4 2025. Stated as a path or vision in every call since Q4 2023 without analytical scaffolding in any of them.
- **"Boy who cried FSD" / self-aware overoptimism followed by identical prediction:** Q2 2023, Q3 2023, Q2 2024, Q3 2024. The acknowledgment of prior error functions as inoculation rather than recalibration — each instance is followed by the same class of prediction without a new mechanism.
- **"Epic / ridiculous / ballistic / bananas":** Q4 2024 ("epic 2026, ridiculous 2027, ballistic 2028"), Q4 2024 ("bananas" applied to $10T Optimus revenue), Q3 2025 ("infinite money glitch"). Aspirational adjectives escalating in each subsequent call.
- **"The single biggest [X] in history":** Q2 2023 ("asset value"), Q4 2023 ("most valuable product of any kind ever, by far"), Q1 2024, Q2 2024 — applied to FSD, Optimus, and autonomous vehicles serially.
- **"We're getting too much in the weeds" / question dismissal:** Q2 2023 (explicit refusal on COGS precision), Q1 2024 (Sacconaghi vehicle strategy question declined entirely), Q4 2024 (KPI question deflected to track record defense), Q2 2025 (lower-cost model engineering deflected via cat analogy).
- **"Amazing abundance" / "sustainable abundance" / "universal high income":** Q4 2025 (mission restatement without explanation of what changed from prior mission). First appearance of escalated utopian language.

### Linguistic precision trend: **Degrading in prepared remarks, flat in CFO Q&A.**

Taneja's disclosure has been the series' most consistent positive signal — specific figures, identified drivers, honest forward cautions. Musk's register has escalated in aspiration over nine quarters, not contracted. The gap between the two speakers is wider in Q4 2025 than in Q2 2023.

## Flags & Risks

### Data Quality Flags
- **Q1 2025 is the most disclosure-deficient quarter in the series.** No revenue, FCF, EPS, or margin figures appear in prepared remarks or Q&A. This is anomalous for a large-cap public company earnings call and was not flagged as an intentional disclosure change.
- **Regulatory credit revenue is a recurring comparability distortion.** Credits ranged from >$2B YTD (Q3 2024) to "record quarter" (Q2 2024) without consistent disclosure of the figure that affects automotive gross margin comparability. Q4 2025 is the first quarter where margin improvement was stated explicitly excluding credits.
- **Bitcoin mark-to-market gains/losses appeared in five of nine quarters** ($600M, $284M, $80M, -$472M disclosed at various points) without any consistent treatment of whether Tesla's Bitcoin position is strategic or speculative.
- **No per-model margin disclosure.** Cybertruck's margin drag was referenced ("excluding Cybertruck, auto margins improved slightly," Q1 2024) without quantification. Q4 2025 effectively retired per-model margin as a reportable metric without providing a replacement.

### Communication-Level Risks
- **The perishable goal pattern is the series' dominant structural risk.** New commitments are introduced each quarter; prior commitments are rarely revisited. The 50% volume CAGR target was retired without being named (Q4 2023). The unboxed manufacturing platform was deprioritized without acknowledgment (Q1 2024). FSD timelines have been extended at least five times across the series. Austin robotaxi launch language has been consistent but the scope ("10 or 20 vehicles on day one") has never been reconciled with "half the U.S. population by year-end" language from the same calls.
- **CEO attention division is a disclosed, unquantified risk.** Q1 2025 included multiple minutes of DOGE commentary and an explicit acknowledgment that Musk's Tesla time allocation would remain reduced through at least May 2025. The Q2 2025 call showed no improvement in analytical engagement.
- **Scope inflation at Q4 2025 is the sharpest single-quarter escalation in the series.** Ten simultaneous capital projects were introduced without prioritization, sequencing, or trade-off framework. The CapEx guidance jump from $9B to ">$20B" in a single quarter — while FCF was $1.4B — is a capital structure flag that was not addressed.
- **Speaker identification failure in Q3 2024.** Multiple substantive Q&A answers came from unidentified representatives. Investors cannot assess accountability of anonymous speakers.
- **FSD licensing pipeline has zero disclosed progress.** First mentioned Q2 2023, elevated to "good chance of a deal this year" in Q1 2024, described as "a few major OEMs" in Q2 2024. No named counterparty, signed agreement, or disclosed term has appeared in any subsequent quarter. By Q4 2025, the topic has dropped from the call narrative entirely.

## Verify Next Quarter

Based on Q4 2025 tracking commitments, the following should be checked when Q1 2026 earnings drops:

1. **CyberCab production start in April 2026.** Musk stated a specific month. Verify: Has production begun? At what initial rate? The acknowledged S-curve ramp should come with a disclosed unit figure, not a directional narrative.

2. **Model S/X wind-down "next quarter" (Q1 2026).** Taneja and Musk indicated S/X production ends in Q1. Verify whether this occurred and whether the Fremont space conversion to Optimus manufacturing has commenced — this is a binary check.

3. **CapEx >$20B for 2026 — first-quarter trajectory.** With FCF at $1.4B in Q4 2025 and a $20B+ CapEx commitment, the financing structure should be disclosed. Were the bank conversations about robotaxi fleet financing referenced by Taneja translated into actual credit facilities?

4. **FSD subscription transition.** Taneja stated the transition to subscription-only FSD begins Q1 2026 and "will impact automotive margins." Verify: Is the margin impact disclosed with a specific dollar or basis-point figure? Does the 1.1 million paid customer base grow, shrink, or hold under the new model?

5. **Robotaxi geographic expansion — city count.** Musk committed to "dozens of major cities by end of year" and "a quarter to half of the U.S." autonomous coverage by end of 2026. Q1 2026 should show at least measurable early progress: named cities, regulatory filings, or fleet size disclosure beyond the "well over 500 vehicles" baseline.

6. **Optimus material factory deployment.** Musk acknowledged in Q4 2025 that Optimus is "not in usage in our factories in a material way." Q1 2026 should be checked for whether this changes and whether a unit figure is disclosed. The gap between the "1,000 units/month" production line claim and "not material" factory usage requires explicit reconciliation.

7. **"Amazing abundance" mission — operational definition.** The mission was updated in Q4 2025 without explanation of what changed or what the prior mission's retirement means for capital allocation. If the Q1 2026 call uses the same phrase without operationalizing it, flag as a FOG escalation signal.
