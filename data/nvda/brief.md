## Candor Trajectory

**Grade progression:** B (3.40) → B (2.65) → B (2.65) → B (3.015) → B (3.25) → B (2.65) → B+ (3.11) → B (2.65) → B (2.65) → B (2.80) → B+ (3.295)

The composite grades mask a structural pattern: **Kress improving, Huang degrading.** CFO financial disclosure has grown more precise and accountable over eleven quarters — the H20 write-down explanation (Q1 2026), the Singapore billing clarification (Q1 2025/Q1 2026), and the voluntary inclusion of SBC in non-GAAP (Q4 2026) represent genuine stewardship improvements. Huang's communication has moved in the opposite direction — from detailed technical education in Q1 2024 toward doctrinal mantra repetition ("compute equals revenues," six times in Q4 2026) and increasingly dismissive treatment of competitive questions.

**Named inflection points:**
- *Q2 2024*: First sustained grade decline; "home run" and "a new computing era has begun" replace data with declaration.
- *Q1 2025*: Partial recovery driven entirely by Kress's H20 write-down transparency; Huang's geopolitical commentary adds a new FOG category.
- *Q3 2025*: Highest-quality quarter on the basis of strategic framework coherence and Kress's $500B visibility disclosure.
- *Q4 2026*: Composite improves to 3.295 on Kress's financial specificity and strategic accountability, while Huang's mantra repetition intensifies — the divergence between the two speakers is at its widest.

**Direction:** Stagnating at B, with internal composition worsening. The headline grade has not broken above B+ on a sustained basis despite revenue growing from $7.19B to $68B.

## FOG Patterns

**Recurring phrases across quarters (with frequency and trajectory):**

1. **"The $1 trillion data center transition"** — Present Q1 2024 through Q4 2026. Never decomposed into NVIDIA-addressable share. Evolved into "$3–4 trillion" framing in Q2 2025 without reconciliation to prior figure. *Degrading: higher numbers, same lack of methodology.*

2. **"At the beginning"** — Q4 FY2024 through Q4 2026; appears at least 4x in Q4 FY2024/Q4 FY2025 calls alone. No milestones defined for what constitutes "middle" or "end." *Static: permanently present regardless of scale.*

3. **"Home run" / "incredible" / "extraordinary"** — Q1 2024 (BlueField "home run"), Q2 2024, Q3 2024, Q4 2026 ("incredibly strong"). Unsupported superlatives applied to products without cited metrics. *Stable pattern.*

4. **"Compute equals revenues"** — Introduced and then repeated six or more times in Q4 2026 alone; not present in earlier quarters in this formulation. *New and intensifying: functions as doctrine, not argument.*

5. **"Every company, every industry, every country"** — Q3 2024, Q4 2024, Q4 FY2024/FY2025. Unfalsifiable TAM expansion. *Stable pattern.*

6. **Competitive dismissiveness without named competitors** — Consistent across all eleven quarters. AMD, Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Broadcom never named directly. ASIC competition addressed via "many startups are created, few go to production" framing (Q2 2025, Q2 2026, Q3 2026). *Degrading: dismissiveness has intensified even as named competitors have grown larger and their revenues more publicly disclosed.*

7. **"A once in a generation opportunity/company"** — Q3 2025 (OpenAI described twice as "once in a generation company"); language then extended to imply multiple investments carry the same descriptor. *New and self-undermining.*

**Linguistic precision:** Kress improving; Huang degrading. The divergence between speakers has widened materially since Q3 2024.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality flags:**
- Quarter labeling is inconsistent across analyses: two separate calls are each labeled "Q4 Fiscal Year 2025" and two are labeled "Q1 Fiscal Year 2026." Investors relying on sequential tracking should verify against actual filing dates.
- Purchase commitments described as "increased significantly" in Q4 2026 without quantification — a material disclosure gap given their relevance to future cash flow.
- Non-GAAP treatment changed in Q4 2026 (SBC inclusion) — prior-period comparisons are not restated in the call.
- Gross margin guidance of "mid-seventies" first committed Q4 FY2024; partially achieved (75% guided Q1 FY2027), but intermediate quarters tracked below target without explicit restatement.

**Communication-level risks (synthesized):**
- **Unfalsifiable demand framing:** Every growth disappointment (China H20 shortfall, gaming supply) is attributed to external factors; every acceleration is attributed to structural demand. No internal execution failures have been voluntarily disclosed in eleven quarters.
- **Capital allocation opacity at scale:** $100B annual FCF, $10B Anthropic investment, Groq acquisition, "significantly increased" purchase commitments — no allocation framework disclosed. Buyback question (Q4 2026) received non-answer for the second consecutive year.
- **China risk normalization:** Moved from "20–25% of Data Center revenue" (Q2 2024) to "not assuming any revenue" (Q3 2025 onward) to "zero revenue generated" (Q4 2026) — a permanent market loss being managed through omission rather than strategic reframing.
- **Customer concentration undisclosed:** "A little over 50%" of Data Center revenue from top CSPs/hyperscalers disclosed Q2 2024; not updated since. At $62B quarterly data center revenue, a 50% concentration among 4–5 buyers is a material undisclosed risk.
- **$500B visibility conflation:** Signed orders, framework agreements, and stated customer intentions are aggregated without delineation. Composition has never been disclosed.
