## Narrative Assessment

The dashboard is registering a Pre-stress regime with two of seven waterfall stages firing, and the pattern of activation matters as much as the count. Stage 1 (Discretionary Cuts) is lit by NKE and LULU — both reporting weak sell-through, markdown pressure, and management language that has shifted to cautious — while Stage 4 (Housing Stress) is triggered by OPEN alone, citing a housing market locked up by 50%-since-2020 price appreciation and elevated mortgage rates compressing transaction volume. The unusual feature of this configuration is that Stages 2 and 3 (Essential Trade-Down and Credit Bridging) remain dark even as Stage 4 fires, which historically would suggest the housing freeze is supply-and-affordability driven rather than a symptom of broad credit deterioration. That read is corroborated by JPM's commentary — "nothing that concerning" in wholesale, consumer performing in line — and by AFRM's NACO curves holding tight. The waterfall is not cascading; it is flickering at the premium-discretionary and housing ends simultaneously, which is consistent with a rate-driven squeeze on aspirational spending rather than the early stages of a cycle turn.

The most consequential signal movement is the divergence between the two discretionary names and the broader consumer cohort. NKE's score has actually been climbing — from 2.71 in Q4-2025 to 3.12 in Q3-2026 — yet it is still firing Stage 1, meaning the improvement is coming off a very stressed base and the absolute signal readings (weak pricing power, promotional digital channel, decelerating capex, management flagging a comeback "taking longer than we would like") remain distress-level. LULU's trajectory is the more alarming of the two: the score dropped sharply to 2.43 in Q4-2025 after reaching 3.00, with explicit trade-down language referencing Canadian consumer sensitivity to markdowns and a stated effort to reduce markdown penetration that management itself acknowledges is incomplete. Against that, WMT's trend line is moving the opposite direction — scoring 3.65 (A) in Q3-2026, the highest reading in the dataset — and WMT is directly calling out income-cohort migration: higher-income consumers are now shopping there. That bifurcation is the clearest stress fingerprint in the data: premium athletic and aspirational home categories are bleeding share to value, but it is not yet showing up in credit or essential goods channels.

On Phase X proximity, the regime needs four or more stages firing to escalate meaningfully. The stages closest to their thresholds are Stage 2 (Essential Trade-Down, currently 0/2) and Stage 3 (Credit Bridging, currently 0/5). WMT's trade-down commentary is directionally pointing at Stage 2, but COST is still scoring 3.38 in Q1-2026 with management tone described as consistent and no markdown language — the essential trade-down signal needs COST or a grocery analog to confirm, not just WMT capturing incremental trips. On the credit side, JPM (3.25, trending down from 3.71 a year ago) and COF (3.12 in Q4-2025, down from 3.65 at the start of the tracked period) are both in multi-quarter score compression. Neither is near a distress threshold, but the direction is unambiguous: both are declining at roughly 40-50 basis points of score per year. If COF's next print falls below 3.00 and JPM follows below 3.25, Stage 3 comes into range for activation by mid-cycle. De-escalation would require NKE's sell-through data to normalize (management has flagged this is in progress but slow) and OPEN to show transaction volume recovering — neither looks imminent given rate levels.

Three things warrant close attention next quarter. First, watch COF's Q1-2026 earnings for charge-off trajectory and any change in forward reserve language — COF has gone from A to B+ over four quarters and is the single most important credit canary for Stage 3 activation; a miss here alongside continued JPM compression would put two of the five Stage 3 tickers in question simultaneously. Second, LULU's Q1-2026 print will reveal whether the markdown reduction effort is gaining traction or whether North American consumer weakness has joined the Canadian signal — if LULU's score stays below 2.50 and NKE does not show further improvement, Stage 1 deepens and begins to look less like category-specific turbulence and more like a broad premiumization reversal. Third, monitor the housing market context signals (HY OAS and 10Y yield fields are currently blank in this dashboard) — OPEN's Stage 4 activation is entirely rate-and-affordability dependent, and any Fed pivot communication or spread tightening would be the most direct catalyst for that stage to go dark, which would drop the waterfall back to a single-stage, clearly-benign configuration.
