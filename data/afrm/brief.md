## FOG Patterns

**Recurring phrases across multiple quarters — no improvement detected:**

1. **"Killer quarter" / "excellent quarter" / "we crush this quarter"** — Appears in Q3 FY2023, Q4 FY2024, Q1 FY2025, Q4 FY2025, Q2 FY2026 prepared remarks. Consistently the opening note, consistently content-free. The prepared remarks section has become the highest-FOG zone precisely because it is the shortest.

2. **"Firing on all pistons"** — Q1 FY2025 and Q4 FY2025. Mechanical cliché with no accompanying specifics.

3. **"Credit is job #1 through #5"** — Q3 FY2023 and cited as a ritual phrase; flags from Q3 FY2023 analysis noted the risk of calcification. The phrase has become a stock reassurance rather than new information.

4. **Strategic deferral framing** — "I don't want to steal the show," "come to the investor forum," "ask me next quarter," "in the next few quarters." Appeared in Q1 FY2024 (investor forum, at least four times), Q3 FY2025 ("ask me next quarter"), Q1 FY2026 ("next quarter"). A commitment made across multiple quarters to disclose direct deposit metrics has never been fulfilled.

5. **Pipeline FOG** — "Quite well filled," "very constructive," "tone and tenor is constructive" — Q4 FY2023 (capital markets), Q2 FY2025 (sales pipeline), Q3 FY2025. No counterparty names, dollar values, or conversion timelines attached.

6. **"A lot of room to grow" / "a lot more things coming"** — Q3 FY2024 (Affirm Card, twice); Q2 FY2024 (Affirm account); Q4 FY2025 (AdaptAI). Vague assertion without timeline or milestone.

7. **"We're dangerously close"** — Q2 FY2024 on reward programs. The reward program has not been confirmed as launched in any subsequent quarter transcript.

**Linguistic precision trend: degrading slightly in prepared remarks, stable in Q&A.** O'Hare has maintained Linford's precision standard in Q&A. Levchin's Q&A remains more specific than his prepared remarks. However, the prepared remarks section — which has shrunk to 90–200 words across recent quarters — has become the FOG concentration point, not an improvement over the full prepared remarks of earlier quarters.

## Flags & Risks

**Data Quality**
- Non-GAAP metrics (RLTC, AOI margin) are the governing financial anchors; GAAP profitability is referenced as a future milestone without a date. The call format systematically directs investors to supplement decks rather than presenting key metrics orally.
- The $30M non-recurring securitization benefit (Q4 FY2025) was voluntarily disclosed by O'Hare — a positive signal — but demonstrates that RLTC can be materially inflated by one-time items without headline acknowledgment.
- No prior-period guidance is formally revisited on any call. Affirm's format structurally eliminates the moment where promises are graded.

**Communication-Level Risks**
- **Serial deferral pattern, confirmed.** The commitment to disclose Debit+/Affirm Card unit economics (Q3 FY2023), direct deposit metrics (Q2 FY2025, Q3 FY2025), and 0% Days results (Q1 FY2026) follows the same pattern: announced, deferred, not systematically closed. Investors maintain their own tracking against management's stated intentions.
- **Departing enterprise merchant.** Referenced as a modeling footnote in Q4 FY2025 and Q2 FY2026 without strategic explanation, counterparty name, or reflection on retention risk. Material enough to restructure the top-five disclosure but treated as a clause.
- **Asymmetric specificity.** Established products (card metrics, ABS spreads) receive quantitative treatment; new verticals (Intuit, rent, agentic commerce, automotive) receive "too early to say" or "put nothing in your model." The trail of unresolved new-vertical promises grows each quarter.
- **Affirm Card as perpetual "next catalyst."** The card has appeared in every quarter since Q3 FY2023 as the primary upside driver. Specific milestones exist ($7,500 / 10M cards), but no timeline is attached and no prior sub-milestones have been formally closed.
- **AI differentiation asserted, not demonstrated.** Across Q4 FY2024, Q3 FY2025, Q2 FY2026: competitive moat is described as ML superiority with specific historical claims ("been at it 13-14 years," "POS v12") but no approval rate, loss rate, or model performance benchmarks against competitors.
- **Guidance floor as permanent posture.** The "just a floor" construction (Q4 FY2023, Q2 FY2024) means management can claim any beat as a success. When guidance is always set to beat, the floor loses information value.
