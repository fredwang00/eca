## 1. Candor Trajectory

**Sector pattern: Both companies are stagnating, but at structurally different floors.**

RKLB has oscillated between 2.65 and 3.125 since Q4 2023, never sustaining improvement above its Q3 2023 peak (3.625 — an anomaly driven by Beck's mission failure disclosure, not a new communication baseline). ASTS has never left the C+ band across twelve quarters, ranging from 2.15 to 2.75 with no directional trend in either direction.

The sector's aggregate candor score — averaging across all 23 quarter-observations — is approximately **2.74**, solidly in the B-/C+ transition zone. Neither company has demonstrated a sustained upward trajectory.

**Outlier quarters:**

| Company | Quarter | Score | Driver |
|---|---|---|---|
| RKLB | Q3 2023 | 3.625 | Anomaly disclosure; prior guidance accountability; Neutron pricing discipline |
| ASTS | Q4 2023 | 2.75 | First manufacturing setback with ownership language; TSMC named; structured CFO cost guidance |
| ASTS | Q3 2024 | 2.15 | Zero commercial revenue; cost revision framed without ownership; financial candor graded flat C |
| RKLB | Q3 2025 | 3.125 | Voluntary Neutron budget overrun disclosure; specific long-term margin targets by segment |

**Structural divergence within ASTS:** The company's CFO has demonstrably improved variance-attribution discipline from Q4 2023 onward, while the CEO/President register is analytically unchanged across all twelve quarters. This bifurcation is now the stable pattern. It is not a drafting problem — it is a deliberate presentation architecture. RKLB shows a milder version of the same dynamic: Beck's Q&A performance consistently outperforms prepared remarks, a pattern flagged since Q2 2023.

**Sector-level conclusion:** Neither company has learned to communicate as a commercially mature entity. Both peaked in disclosure quality at moments of operational stress (RKLB: mission failure; ASTS: manufacturing setback) and reverted afterward. Candor improves reactively in this sector, not proactively.

## 3. Bottleneck Migration

**The dominant constraint language has shifted, but not uniformly across companies.**

| Period | RKLB Dominant Constraint | ASTS Dominant Constraint |
|---|---|---|
| Q1–Q3 2023 | Capital (Neutron funding); supply chain (COVID tail) | Capital (existential liquidity); satellite supply chain |
| Q4 2023 – Q2 2024 | Neutron schedule (supplier failures, tank design) | Manufacturing (supplier failures, ASIC tape-out); capital |
| Q3 2024 – Q1 2025 | Neutron schedule (budget overrun, AFP machine); Space Systems pipeline opacity | Per-satellite cost escalation; launch slot availability |
| Q2 2025 – Q4 2025 | Neutron schedule (Q4 2026 target); regulatory (Mynaric/Germany); M&A integration | Manufacturing pace vs. cadence targets; spectrum (Ligado/L-Band); regulatory (FCC) |

**Sector-level bottleneck reading:**

- **Capital** was the shared existential constraint through mid-2023 for both companies. Both have since resolved acute liquidity concerns (RKLB: >$1B cash by Q3 2025; ASTS: $3.9B pro forma by Q4 2025).
- **Manufacturing** replaced capital as the binding constraint for ASTS from Q4 2023 onward and remains so. The per-satellite cost drift ($16–18M → $21–23M, a 20–45% increase) and CapEx guidance misses (Q4 2025: ~$82–132M overage) are manufacturing-execution signals, not demand signals.
- **Schedule / engineering** is the persistent constraint for RKLB's Neutron program. The constraint language shifted from "supplier failure" (Q4 2023) to "tank design" (Q1–Q2 2024) to "AFP machine qualification" (Q3–Q4 2024) to "tank rebuild" (Q3–Q4 2025). Each quarter names a different proximate cause; the underlying pattern is that the integration of novel manufacturing technology (AFP) into a first-stage vehicle is the durable constraint, not any single supplier.
- **Regulatory** has emerged as a new constraint in Q2–Q4 2025 for both companies: RKLB's Mynaric acquisition (German court), ASTS's spectrum strategy (FCC on L-Band, Ligado). This is a new bottleneck category not present in 2023 transcripts.
- **Power and chip supply** — the dominant AI-sector bottleneck vocabulary — are **absent** from both companies' constraint language. This sector is not experiencing GPU or power scarcity; its constraints are manufacturing throughput, engineering schedule, and spectrum/regulatory.

## 4. Cross-Company Corroboration

### Corroborated signals (both companies, independent sourcing):

**Government demand is real and growing.**
- RKLB: SDA Tranche 2 ($489M), SDA Tranche 3 (confirmed, value undisclosed), MACH-TB 2.0 vehicle, HASTE pipeline described as active across thirteen quarters.
- ASTS: SDA $43M + $30M contracts; government revenue described as "hundreds of millions annually" target; non-communications applications referenced.
- **Assessment:** Two independent companies are both converting government pipeline to signed contracts. The U.S. government (SDA in particular) is the most corroborated demand signal in this dataset.

**Capital markets are accessible to space companies with demonstrated technical milestones.**
- RKLB raised >$1B in equity across Q1–Q3 2025 (including $92.8M Q1, $303.8M ATM Q2). Backlog reached $1.85B by Q4 2025.
- ASTS raised over $2B in equity between Q3 2024 and Q4 2025, with AT&T, Google, Verizon, and Vodafone converting notes to equity in Q4 2025. Strategic investors are publicly named.
- **Assessment:** Both companies experienced liquidity constraint in 2023 and both resolved it by 2025. Capital access corroborates that institutional investors and strategic counterparties view the sector as fundable, not speculative-terminal.

**Manufacturing complexity is systematically underestimated at program inception.**
- RKLB: Neutron budget grew from $250–300M to ~$360M (+30%); timeline slipped by at least two years from original estimates.
- ASTS: Per-satellite cost grew from $16–18M to $21–23M (+20–45%); CapEx guidance missed in five of the last six quarters.
- **Assessment:** Both programs independently exhibit the same pattern: early estimates significantly understate actual costs and timelines. This is a sector-level pattern, not a company-specific failure.

### Unverified claims (single-company, counterparty silent or absent):

**RKLB's constellation vision.** Twelve consecutive quarters of discussion with zero external corroboration — no named investor partner, no anchor customer, no regulatory filing. The counterparty (whoever would fund or contract for the constellation) has never been named.

**ASTS's "~50 MNO partners."** Across twelve quarters, only four partners have disclosed binding commercial agreements (AT&T, Verizon, Vodafone, stc). The remaining ~46 "partners" have no external corroboration. This figure has appeared in every quarter since Q1 2023 without a single additional named partner conversion.

**ASTS's government revenue claims.** The "$13B PLEO expansion opportunity" and "hundreds of millions annually" government targets appear without named agency, program, or RFP. The SDA contracts ($43M + $30M) are real but represent a fraction of the claimed opportunity. The gap between disclosed contract value and claimed opportunity size is unverified.

**RKLB's Golden Dome and Golden Dome-adjacent positioning.** The $175B program is publicly real; RKLB's role is not corroborated by any government announcement, RFP selection, or subcontractor disclosure.

## 6. Aggregated Flags & Risks

### Risks appearing across both companies:

**1. CapEx guidance accuracy is poor and deteriorating.**
- RKLB: Neutron budget overran by 30%+; the $250–300M estimate at IPO reached ~$360M by Q3 2025, with the program not yet complete.
- ASTS: CapEx missed guidance in five of six recent quarters; Q4 2025 actual was $82–132M above the $275–325M guided range. Each overage is framed as an acceleration, never as a forecasting failure.
- **Sector signal:** Both companies systematically underguide CapEx and over-attribute misses to positive decisions. Investors modeling capital consumption from guidance are receiving inputs that consistently understate actual spend.

**2. New strategic themes are introduced without prior commitments being retired.**
- RKLB introduced five new narratives in Q4 2025 alone (silicon solar, data centers, MTO, Golden Dome, Rocket Lab Europe). None of the prior multi-quarter narratives (constellation, HASTE scaling, $320B TAM) were revisited or closed.
- ASTS introduced AI/radar/on-orbit compute/spectrum monetization in Q4 2025. The "~50 MNO partners" framing, unchanged since Q1 2023, remains active without resolution.
- **Sector signal:** Narrative proliferation without commitment retirement is a perishable-goal management pattern. Both companies are running it simultaneously.

**3. Competitive non-engagement.**
- RKLB has not quantified its Electron market share or named competitors across any quarter in the dataset.
- ASTS has not named Starlink Direct-to-Cell in ten consecutive calls, despite it being the most direct competitive threat to their core value proposition.
- **Sector signal:** Both companies describe their competitive positions without reference to named competition. This is a FOG pattern, not a strategic discipline.

**4. Government opportunity is claimed but incompletely anchored.**
- Both companies describe multi-billion-dollar government opportunities. Both have signed contracts well below the claimed opportunity size. Neither has provided a conversion timeline, agency roadmap, or RFP pipeline that would allow external verification.

### Risks isolated to one company:

**RKLB only:**
- Mynaric regulatory deflection ("don't believe everything you read") on a material pending transaction.
- Neutron timeline has slipped at least four times (2024 → mid-2025 → H2 2025 → early 2026 → Q4 2026) without a systematic post-mortem on forecasting methodology.
- Solero/SolAero margin target acknowledged as "a couple of quarters late" in two separate quarters without resolving the original miss.
- Q4 2025 non-GAAP gross margin guidance cited as "9% to 41%" (transcription error for "39%–41%"); uncorrected in prepared remarks.

**ASTS only:**
- Manufacturing pace miss not confirmed or denied when raised directly by an analyst (Bryan Kraft, Q4 2025). Accountability loop broken.
- "$1B in contracted revenue commitments" cited without customer mapping, duration weighting, or distinction between received prepayments and contractual minimums.
- Ligado/L-Band $420M SPV obligation due October 2025 — Q4 2025 call did not confirm whether payment was made on schedule.
- Revenue recognition basis never disaggregated across $70.9M full-year 2025 actuals; gateway, government, and MNO commercial proportions undisclosed.
- Pro forma cash ($3.9B) blends actual balance sheet with future available liquidity (February 2026 convertible proceeds, undrawn ATM) without isolating the actual December 31, 2025 cash balance.

**The Gerstner Check:**

The pundit narrative on the space sector in 2025 centers on two claims: (1) commercial space is approaching inflection, and (2) both companies are on a path to profitability within two years.

**The bottom-up numbers challenge both claims:**

- RKLB's $602M in 2025 revenue with gross margins reaching 37–38% by Q4 is a real positive signal — this is genuine margin expansion from 11.6% in Q1 2023. But FCF burns of $(69.4M) in Q3 alone (and likely similar in Q4) indicate that Neutron development cash consumption is not yet in the denominator of the margin story. The company is simultaneously expanding its legacy business margins and consuming capital at an accelerating rate on a next-generation vehicle that is running 30%+ over budget and two-plus years behind schedule.
- ASTS recognized $70.9M in revenue in all of 2025, against a cash/liquidity position of $3.9B (pro forma). The capital-to-revenue ratio is extraordinary — approximately 55:1 — and the $150–200M 2026 revenue guidance, if achieved, would still leave the company burning cash at a rate orders of magnitude above revenue. The "$1B annual revenue approaching 2027" claim requires a 14x revenue expansion in two years from a manufacturing and deployment base that has consistently missed its own intermediate targets.

**The sector is not approaching profitability inflection on a two-year horizon.** RKLB might achieve FCF breakeven on its launch/space-systems legacy business if Neutron spending is excluded; it has guided for FCF positivity in 2027. ASTS has framed cash-flow-positive as "25 satellites on operating basis, excluding CapEx" — a metric designed to exclude the primary cash expenditure. The pundit claim that the space sector is approaching profitability conflates gross margin improvement (RKLB, real) with FCF discipline (neither company, not yet present).
