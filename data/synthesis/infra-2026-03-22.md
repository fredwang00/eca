## Signal 1 — Candor Trajectory

**Sector pattern: Both companies plateau at B, spike once, and revert. The spike is driven by a named hyperscaler contract. The reversion is structural.**

| Company | Direction | Peak Quarter | Peak Driver | Current State |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| IREN | Gradual improvement with one inflection + one reversal | Q1 FY2026 (B+, 3.015) | Microsoft contract specificity | Q2 FY2026: B (2.65) — reversion |
| CIFR | Slight decline | Q3 2025 (B+, 3.05) | AWS/Google named counterparties | Q4 2025: B (2.35) — financial data collapse |

**The pattern is identical in structure, opposite in trigger:** IREN's B+ quarter was driven by *disclosing* a hyperscaler contract with full economics. CIFR's B+ quarter was driven by *announcing* hyperscaler contracts — then declined when the next call produced zero financial data to substantiate execution.

**What is not improving across either company:**
- Prepared-remarks FOG (IREN: Dimensions 3–4 at C/C+ every quarter; CIFR: superlative-first sequencing in Q3 prepared remarks)
- Accountability scaffolding — neither company runs an explicit "we said X; here is X's status" review
- Aspirational/signed conflation in pipeline language

**Outlier (positive):** IREN's Q1 FY2026 disclosure standard — named counterparty, total contract value, annual value, margin, IRR scenarios, prepayment terms — is the highest-precision single-quarter disclosure in this dataset. It is also a one-quarter event, not a sustained behavior change.

**Outlier (negative):** CIFR's Q4 2025 "business update" call — zero revenue, zero cash flow, zero CapEx, zero balance sheet data — while mid-execution on a multi-billion-dollar construction cycle is the lowest-quality financial disclosure quarter in this dataset, including IREN's Q4 FY2024 trough.

**Structural finding:** Neither company's candor score is on a durable upward trajectory. Both oscillate around a B floor, with scores driven primarily by whether a named external counterparty (Microsoft, AWS, Google) forces disclosure specificity that management would not have volunteered.

## Signal 3 — Bottleneck Migration

Only two companies with data; longitudinal signal limited but directionally clear.

**IREN bottleneck migration (9 quarters):**

| Period | Dominant Constraint Language |
|---|---|
| Q3 FY2023 – Q2 FY2024 | **Funding / balance sheet** — $626M funding plan, ATM raises, ELOC structures; survival-mode capital |
| Q3 FY2024 – Q4 FY2024 | **Hash rate scaling / ASIC supply** — Bitmain amendments, T21→S21 Pro, site build-out |
| Q1 FY2025 – Q2 FY2025 | **Power / interconnection** — 2.3 GW ERCOT agreements; "without signed interconnection agreements, quantum of MW timing and cost is highly uncertain" |
| Q3 FY2025 – Q1 FY2026 | **GPU supply and customer contracting** — "demand is not the constraint"; GPU financing as the critical path |
| Q2 FY2026 | **Execution / transition management** — simultaneous ASIC-to-GPU transition at three sites; term loan structure; 23% sequential revenue decline context |

**CIFR bottleneck (2 quarters):**

| Period | Dominant Constraint Language |
|---|---|
| Q3 2025 | **Customer contracting and financing** — $5.5B AWS signed; construction financing "in coming days and weeks" unresolved |
| Q4 2025 | **ERCOT interconnection + construction execution** — batch process finalization; Barber Lake build; "the pace has been pretty frantic" |

**Cross-company corroboration on bottleneck migration:** Both companies independently confirm that **power/interconnection** was the dominant constraint language in 2024–early 2025, and both have shifted to **GPU supply, construction execution, and customer contracting** as the 2025–2026 constraint. Neither company is currently leading with chip availability as the binding constraint — both frame customer demand as strong and supply-side logistics (GPU delivery, construction timelines, financing draw schedules) as the operative variable.

**Notably absent from both transcripts:** Memory constraints, labor shortages, and permitting (beyond ERCOT queue) as named bottlenecks. Power remains a background constraint but is no longer the *lead* constraint in either company's language.

## Signal 4 — Cross-Company Corroboration

### Corroborated Signals (Both Companies Independently Confirm)

**1. Hyperscaler demand is real and binding.** IREN signed Microsoft ($9.7B, 5-year). CIFR signed AWS ($5.5B, 15-year) and Google-backstopped Fluidstack ($1.4B). Three named hyperscalers — Microsoft, AWS, Google — across two companies in the same sector, in the same approximate window. This is not promotional language; these are contracted obligations with named counterparties and disclosed terms. **Corroboration strength: High.**

**2. West Texas / ERCOT as preferred geography.** Both companies are building or holding capacity in Texas/ERCOT. IREN has Sweetwater and Horizon 1 Childress; CIFR has McLennan, Macassa, Colchis, and the "West Texas will be the data center capital of the world" declaration. Independent geographic convergence on the same region. **Corroboration strength: Moderate** — both companies are corroborating each other, but neither is an independent validator of the other's specific site value.

**3. Power/interconnection signed commitments as a differentiator.** IREN voluntarily introduced the signed/aspirational distinction for interconnection language in Q1 FY2025. CIFR's ERCOT framing (queue participation, batch finalization) is less explicit but reinforces that interconnection position is treated as a moat claim. **Corroboration strength: Moderate.**

**4. GPU financing at scale is available and sub-6%.** IREN disclosed $3.6B at sub-6% from Goldman/JPMorgan. CIFR referenced project bonds "trading well above par." Two data points suggesting institutional debt markets have opened for GPU-secured infrastructure at reasonable rates. **Corroboration strength: Moderate** — CIFR's evidence is qualitative only.

### Unverified / Single-Source Claims

**1. IREN's Sweetwater 1.4 GW pipeline.** Six consecutive quarters of "active conversations" with zero named counterparties. No independent corroboration from any counterparty (Microsoft has not mentioned Sweetwater; no other company references it). **Flag: Unverified, duration exceeding credibility threshold.**

**2. CIFR's 3.2 GW total pipeline.** No stage breakdown, no named tenants beyond the signed 544 MW. The gap between 544 MW contracted and 3.2 GW "pipeline" is not validated by any external source in this dataset. **Flag: Unverified.**

**3. CIFR's "West Texas will be the data center capital of the world."** Stated prediction without cited demand forecasts, infrastructure maps, or competitive analysis. IREN corroborates Texas as an active geography, but this specific superlative is a single-source assertion. **Flag: Aspirational, unverifiable.**

**4. IREN's $200–250M annualized AI cloud revenue by December 2025.** Set August 2025; not audited on Q2 FY2026 call. No independent corroboration. **Flag: Unaudited commitment.**

**5. Both companies claim demand is "not the constraint."** IREN: "demand is not the constraint" (multiple quarters). CIFR: "multiple hyperscalers in data rooms" at Ulysses. These claims are corroborated by each other but not by a named third party declining a deal due to capacity shortage. Self-corroboration is not independent validation. **Flag: Mutually reinforcing, not independently verified.**

## Signal 5 — Sector FOG Index

**Finding: Both companies deploy the same vocabulary from the same register. The FOG is sector-wide, not company-specific.**

### Phrase Clustering Across Both Companies

| Phrase Category | IREN Instances | CIFR Instances | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Strong / very strong / extremely strong demand" | Every AI quarter, Q2 FY2024 – Q2 FY2026; never quantified | "Strong momentum," "strong demand signals" — Q3, Q4 2025 | **Sector-wide; zero quantification in either company** |
| "Uniquely positioned" | Q3 FY2025, Q4 FY2025, Q1 FY2026, Q2 FY2026 | Implicit in "Cipher has officially arrived as a leader" | Circular self-positioning without comparative data |
| "Active / advanced discussions / conversations" | Sweetwater + Horizon 1; 6+ quarters | Ulysses ("multiple hyperscalers in data rooms"); Stingray ("preferred tenant") | **Sector-wide; functions to claim progress without disclosing terms** |
| Excited/transformative/arrived | "Super excited" — 8+ instances Q2 FY2024 alone | "Truly transformative," "officially arrived as a leader" | Identical emotional register; zero information content |
| Self-declared leadership | "Best-in-class" without comparables | "Cipher has officially arrived as a leader in the HPC revolution" | Both companies confer leadership on themselves |
| Pipeline confidence without stage | "$3.4B ARR" with ~$1.1B uncontracted | "3.2 GW pipeline" with no LOI/deposit/ERCOT breakdown | **Sector-wide; aggregate pipeline figures without quality ladders** |

**Sector FOG Assessment:** Both companies use virtually identical language patterns in prepared remarks. The phrases are interchangeable — a prepared-remarks paragraph from IREN's Q2 FY2026 call and one from CIFR's Q3 2025 call could be swapped without detection by a reader who didn't know the company name.

**The one structural exception:** IREN's Roberts voluntarily introduced the signed/aspirational distinction for interconnection disclosures (Q1 FY2025 onward) and maintained it consistently. This is a genuine above-sector-average practice on one specific metric. It has not been extended to customer pipeline language, and CIFR has no equivalent practice. This exception does not offset the overall sector FOG pattern.

**Uniformity verdict:** 2 of 2 covered companies use "strong demand" or equivalent without quantification in every AI-era call. 2 of 2 use pipeline figures without stage breakdowns. 2 of 2 use self-declared leadership claims. For a two-company sample, this is 100% uniformity — a red flag per the sector FOG definition. The uniformity suggests coordinated industry narrative rather than independent evidence. The underlying demand is real (hyperscaler contracts corroborate it), but the *language* is performing rather than reporting.

## Signal 6 — Aggregated Flags & Risks

### Risks Appearing Across Both Companies

**1. Pipeline confidence outrunning signed contracts.**
IREN presents $3.4B ARR target with ~32% uncontracted and equivalent confidence to the signed portion. CIFR presents 3.2 GW pipeline with ~83% uncontracted and no stage breakdown. Both companies' investment theses depend on a conversion rate from aspirational to signed that neither company has disclosed historically or prospectively.

**2. Accountability scaffolding absent.**
Neither company runs an explicit prior-quarter commitment review on earnings calls. IREN's December 2025 AI cloud revenue target ($200–250M annualized) was set in August 2025 and not audited in February 2026. CIFR's Q3 2025 promise of Barber Lake financing "in the coming days and weeks" was not revisited in Q4 2025. Investors must self-track — management does not.

**3. Financial disclosure quality inversely correlated with capital activity.**
IREN's lowest-quality disclosure quarter (Q4 FY2024, C+) coincided with its largest ATM raise ($823M, 121M shares). CIFR's lowest-quality disclosure quarter (Q4 2025, B but zero financials) coincides with active multi-billion-dollar construction financing. In both cases, the quarters where investors most need financial data are the quarters where management most avoids providing it.

**4. ERCOT/West Texas exposure at scale.**
Both companies have material capacity in ERCOT. IREN: Sweetwater (1.4 GW), Horizon 1 Childress; CIFR: McLennan, Macassa, Colchis. Both frame ERCOT positioning as a competitive advantage. Neither company discloses the cost of ERCOT queue delays already incurred, nor do they provide probability-weighted power availability timelines. ERCOT batch process finalization risk is a shared sector exposure that neither company treats as a downside scenario.

**5. Aspirational language structurally unchanged by disclosure events.**
IREN signed Microsoft in Q1 FY2026 — a landmark event that improved that quarter's candor score. By Q2 FY2026, promotional language in prepared remarks was unchanged. CIFR signed AWS in Q3 2025 — then Q4 2025 produced a "business update" with no financial data. In both cases, signing a major contract did not produce sustained improvement in overall disclosure quality. The contracts improved one dimension; the structural FOG patterns are unchanged.

### Risks Isolated to One Company

**IREN-specific:**
- **Sweetwater duration risk:** Six consecutive quarters of identical pipeline language without a named counterparty. This is a perishable narrative — each additional quarter without a contract reduces rather than maintains credibility.
- **Co-CEO absence (Q2 FY2026):** Will Roberts entirely absent from a foundational transition quarter. Unexplained.
- **Data quality errors:** Convertible note maturity stated as June 2023 (in the past); "1 million" vs. "$1 billion" convertible notes. Neither corrected on-call.
- **ATM dilution vs. stated low appetite:** $823M raised from 121M shares not reconciled against Roberts' stated low appetite for dilution in AI context.
- **23% sequential revenue decline (Q2 FY2026):** Not acknowledged as a challenge; framed entirely as managed transition with no friction disclosed.
- **IFRS restatement (Q3 FY2025):** Operating-to-investing cash flow reclassification creates discontinuity in multi-period comparisons.

**CIFR-specific:**
- **Q4 2025 financial blackout:** Zero revenue, FCF, CapEx, or balance sheet data from a company mid-cycle on billions in construction. If this pattern repeats in Q1 2026, Dimension 1 should be revised to F.
- **New CFO disclosure style unestablished:** Mumford's Q3 was his first call. Q4 produced no financial data, making pattern assessment impossible after two quarters.
- **Stingray credibility overhang:** Page's explicit confidence language ("I would not be speaking so confidently if I were not so confident") sets a near-term verifiable commitment. Non-signing is a named credibility event.
- **Construction financing specificity absent:** $5.5B AWS obligation announced with construction financing "to be announced in coming days and weeks" — not resolved through two quarters of calls.

### The Gerstner Check

**Pundit claim to check:** "Hyperscaler AI infrastructure spending is driving hundreds of billions in new supply-side investment."

**What the bottom-up data shows for this two-company sample:**
- ~$17B in signed revenue contracts (named hyperscalers: Microsoft, AWS, Google)
- ~$8.8B in forward CapEx committed or in-process
- ~$5.4B in disclosed debt financing raised to fund it
- Current combined quarterly revenue of ~$257M — still predominantly Bitcoin mining

**The gap:** These two companies are real participants in hyperscaler demand, with named contracts from three of the largest technology companies on earth. But current revenue is ~$257M/quarter combined. The $17B in contracts converts over 5–15 year terms. The near-term (2025–2026) capital deployment is real and substantial, but the revenue inflection is 12–24 months away by the companies' own disclosed timelines. Sector-level claims about AI infrastructure demand are corroborated by contract evidence. Sector-level claims about *current revenue generation* from AI infrastructure are not — both companies are still primarily Bitcoin mining operations transitioning under signed but not-yet-revenue-generating hyperscaler contracts.
