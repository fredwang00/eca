## Key Commitments

### Signed
- **Fluidstack/Google deal:** 300 gross MW, $3B over 10 years, Google backstopping $1.4B of Fluidstack obligations plus warrants representing ~5.4% pro forma equity. Named counterparties, specific term, quantified backstop.
- **AWS deal:** 300 gross MW, $5.5B contracted revenue over 15-year term, Phase 1 delivery July 2026, Phase 2 Q4 2026, rent commencing August 2026. Named counterparty, specific phasing, revenue figure.
- **Odessa PPA (Luminant):** Fixed-price power at ~$0.027/kWh, behind-the-meter, disclosed through July 2027 expiration. Operationally substantiated across ten consecutive quarters.
- **Fortress financing partnership:** Announced Q1 2025 as "signed in the last few days." Structure: Cipher retains ~40% economics without contributing capital; Fortress backstops construction. Final terms deferred pending lease signing.
- **Black Pearl site acquisition:** $7M for 300MW ERCOT-approved site (disclosed Q3 2023). 50-year ground lease. Phase 1 energized Q2 2025.
- **Bitmain rig contracts:** 45%/45% payment structure on delivery, with specific Q1/Q2 2024 delivery tranches disclosed. S21 rig purchase with one-third Q1, two-thirds Q2 delivery stated Q3 2023.
- **Colchis acquisition:** Named site, disclosed Q3 2025. Power availability targeted 2028, ERCOT approval pending.

### Aspirational
- **HPC pipeline of 3.2 GW across multiple sites** — no binding tenant agreements on Stingray, Reveille, Ulysses, or the three M-sites (McLennan, Macassa, Milsing) as of Q4 2025. Interconnect approvals pending on multiple.
- **"Several ongoing reviews" of M&A opportunities** — cited across Q1–Q2 2024 without named targets, deal sizes, or criteria. No transactions announced.
- **Barber Lake 56 MW optionality** — Page stated "highly confident we will have some sort of deal there pretty soon" (Q3 2025, Q1 2026). No signed agreement disclosed as of final quarter analyzed.
- **Sub-$0.03/kWh at Black Pearl** — contingent on ancillary services monetization and active front-of-meter trading. Described as a scenario, not a contracted rate.
- **HPC tenant at Barber Lake "this year"** — stated Q1 2025, reiterated Q2 2025. No signed lease disclosed through Q1 2026.
- **"We anticipate being the leading Bitcoin miner in the world"** — no definition of "leading," no metric, no timeline. Repeated across multiple quarters.
- **23.5 EH/s by end of 2025** — introduced Q3 2023, qualified in Q3 2024 as subject to HPC repurposing of Black Pearl. Effective suspension without formal withdrawal.

## FOG Patterns

**Recurring phrases across quarters:**
- **"Best-in-class unit economics"** — appears in every quarter from Q1 2023 through Q4 2024. Never benchmarked against named competitors with cited data. The only partial substantiation is the all-in electricity cost per Bitcoin figure, which is specific but never explicitly compared to disclosed peer figures.
- **"We are very excited"** — variants appear in Q1 2023, Q2 2023, Q3 2024, Q4 2024, Q3 2025, and Q1 2026. Peaks in Q4 2024 with "we have never been more excited about the commercial potential."
- **"Tremendous/robust pipeline"** — introduced Q2 2024 with the 1.7 GW figure; escalates to "tremendous pipeline" in Q1 2025 and "robust pipeline" in Q1 2026. Pipeline quality laddering (signed vs. LOI vs. exploratory) is never provided in prepared remarks; requires analyst interrogation.
- **"Multiple data rooms / advanced discussions / several under NDA"** — appears from Q1 2023 (expansion sites) through Q2 2025 (HPC tenants). Functions as a confidence signal without verifiable content. "Pretty advanced" (Q3 2024) and "reasonably far along" (Q2 2025) are structurally identical claims issued across consecutive quarters without resolution.
- **"Uniquely positioned / best-in-class team / best positioned miner"** — superlative cluster present in every quarter. Never falsified, never benchmarked, never withdrawn even as strategy pivots from mining to HPC.
- **"Officially arrived as a leader"** — debuts Q3 2025 and is the most inflated instance in the series, applied to a company that has not yet recognized a dollar of HPC revenue.

**Linguistic precision trajectory:** Improved Q1–Q1 2024 (CEO Q&A specifically), then degraded Q2 2024–Q4 2024 as HPC pivot introduced aspirational content without contractual grounding. Q3 2025 is the high-water mark for factual content (named counterparties, dollar terms, signed contracts). Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 show a two-speed pattern: CFO prepared remarks carry low FOG, CEO prepared remarks carry high FOG, Q&A sits between. The split is now structural and persistent.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality flags:**
- **Q2 2024 CFO arithmetic error:** Farrell described a net loss as both "a sequential decrease of 138%" and "a 16% decrease from the prior quarter" for the same sequential comparison (–$13M to –$15M). Left unresolved during the call. Investors directed to 10-Q for reconciliation.
- **Depreciation policy change (5-year to 3-year, Q2 2024):** Applied prospectively only, increasing D&A by 41% sequentially and 77% year-over-year. No retrospective impact quantification provided. Timing coincides with significant HPC pivot narrative investment.
- **Non-GAAP gap widening:** GAAP-to-adjusted spread grows materially through the series. Q3 2023: –$17.7M GAAP vs. +$5.9M non-GAAP. Q4 2024: –$45M GAAP loss vs. +$107M adjusted for the full year. Five simultaneous exclusions (D&A, SBC, warrant liability, deferred tax, PPA fair value) without call-level explanation of which adjustments reflect economic reality.
- **Q4 2025 financial data absence:** "Business update" framing produced a call with essentially no income statement, cash flow, or capital allocation data despite an active multi-hundred-megawatt construction cycle.
- **Total equity declining:** $805.5M (Q4 2025) vs. $783.2M (Q3 2025) despite $1.3B convertible raise in Q3 2025.

**Communication-level risks:**
- **SoftBank exclusivity disclosed only under analyst pressure (Q1 2025):** A flagship potential tenant relationship lapsed from exclusivity without proactive disclosure. Material information withheld from prepared remarks.
- **Financing opacity at maximum capital deployment:** As of Q1 2026, construction financing for both the AWS and Fluidstack/Google deals remains undisclosed. Management has committed to "updating the market in short order" across two consecutive calls without delivery.
- **Perishable goal accumulation:** The series shows a pattern of aspirational milestones that are quietly replaced rather than closed out—7.2 EH/s target (partially met, then displaced by HPC), 23.5 EH/s 2025 target (suspended without formal withdrawal), Barber Lake tenant "this year" (stated Q1 2025, unresolved through Q1 2026), Fortress exclusivity (window expired, successor arrangement undisclosed).
- **New CFO (Greg Mumford) communication quality:** First prepared remarks are substantially boilerplate. The CFO communication quality established by Ed Farrell—specific, layered, proactively disclosing complexity—is not yet replicated by the successor.
