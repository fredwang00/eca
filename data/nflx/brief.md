## Key Commitments

### Signed
- **WWE Raw:** 52-week live programming deal, commencing January 2025. Cited as fitting within the $17 billion 2024 content envelope. No per-deal economics disclosed despite two direct analyst requests in Q4 2023. Q4 2024 confirmed approximately 2× the linear audience (5 million first-week views).
- **NFL Christmas Day games:** Named deal, Q4 2024 and Q4 2025. Q4 2024 described as record viewership; no licensing cost disclosed.
- **Warner Bros. Discovery acquisition:** $9.7 billion deal disclosed Q4 2025. Neumann quantified a "half a percentage point drag from expected M&A expenses" embedded in 2026 margin guidance. Regulatory approval characterized as certain by Sarandos; no conditions or timeline disclosed.
- **TF1 partnership:** Named Q2 2025. Peters identified selection criteria (territory scale, leading local programmer, deal structure alignment). No financials disclosed.
- **Content market commitments:** "$1 billion commitment to production in Mexico," "$2.5 billion committed to Korean content in Korea," and a U.K. commitment — all named Q1 2025 with dollar figures.

### Aspirational
- Advertising revenue "roughly doubling" annually: cited in Q4 2024, Q1 2025, Q2 2025, Q3 2025, and Q4 2025. No base absolute figure disclosed in any quarter, making the claim unverifiable in magnitude. Q4 2025 provided the first partial anchor: "roughly doubling... to about $3 billion" in 2026.
- "Critical scale" in all 12 advertising countries by 2025: named Q3 2024. Definition of "critical scale" was never quantified by member count, reach percentage, or CPM threshold.
- Games investment scaling "based on demonstrated value to members and returns to the business": named across Q2 2024, Q1 2025, Q3 2025, Q4 2025. No specific proof-point threshold or metric ever defined.
- Live events strategy as "ownable big breakthrough events" rather than full-season rights: asserted Q4 2023, Q3 2024, Q2 2025, Q3 2025. Language has softened incrementally — Q4 2024: "if there was a path where we could make the economics work... we certainly would explore."
- "$43–44 billion" 2025 revenue, raised to "$44.8–45.2 billion" in Q2 2025: operational guidance, not a binding contract.

## FOG Patterns

FOG is structurally concentrated in Sarandos's prepared remarks across all nine quarters. Peters drifts into FOG in transitions and summations. Neumann is the cleanest speaker throughout.

**Recurring phrases across multiple quarters:**

- *"Multi-year build"* / *"multi-year progress"* — Q2 2023, Q3 2023, Q4 2023. A durable excuse that resets the accountability clock each quarter.
- *"Continual improvement"* / *"constantly iterating"* — Q2 2023, Q3 2024, Q2 2025. Motion-without-destination language cited in every advertising and product answer.
- *"We've got a lot of work to do"* — Q2 2023 (twice in advertising section), Q3 2023. Accurate but informationally empty.
- *"Flywheel"* — Q1 2024, Q2 2024, Q4 2024, Q4 2025. Deployed as a conclusion rather than an explanation in every instance.
- *"We're only 6–7% of the addressable market"* — Q4 2023, Q2 2024, Q3 2024, Q4 2024, Q3 2025, Q4 2025. Functions as a permanently available optimism anchor rather than a dynamic statement about current conditions. Its reflexive deployment across every quarter is itself a FOG marker.
- *"We feel good about our progress"* / *"energized as ever"* — Q3 2025 and Q4 2025 in near-identical phrasing.
- *"Thrilling our members"* — Q1 2024, Q3 2024, Q4 2025. Used as a strategic claim without a measurement reference in any instance.

**Trend:** FOG precision did not improve over the nine-quarter span. The Q4 2024 and Q1 2025 calls scored B on the FOG dimension — the high-water mark — driven by Peters's advertising roadmap specificity and Neumann's variance explanations. Q3 2025 regressed to C. Q4 2025 landed at B− despite the theatrical reversal's candor. The structural pattern — specific CFO, mixed Peters, promotional Sarandos — has been stable since Q2 2023.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality flags:**
- Free cash flow is inconsistently disclosed. Q1 2024 and Q2 2024 both cite ~$6B guidance, Q1 2025 reconfirms $8B. Q3 2024, Q3 2025, and Q4 2025 provide no FCF figure despite this being a central investment thesis metric and despite the company having previously spent years educating investors on cash vs. P&L content spend. The Q4 2025 FCF omission is the most notable given the scale of the WB transaction.
- Advertising revenue is reported exclusively as a growth rate (doubling) without an absolute base figure across Q4 2023 through Q3 2025. Q4 2025 is the first call to provide a partial anchor ("roughly doubling to about $3 billion" in 2026), which implies a 2025 base of approximately $1.5 billion — never previously disclosed.
- Quarterly subscriber counts were retired after Q1 2024. The stated replacement — engagement reports, milestone announcements, annual revenue ranges — has not provided equivalent accountability infrastructure. The "primary quality metric" referenced repeatedly in Q4 2025 is explicitly withheld from investors.

**Communication-level risks:**
- **Voluntary problem disclosure is structurally absent.** Across nine quarters, the only self-identified negative surfaced proactively was Neumann's Q3 2025 CIDE tax disclosure (triggered by a material court ruling, not discretion) and Sarandos's animation candor in Q1 2025 ("we've had some misses"). No call contains an unprompted "here is where we fell short" moment on strategy, content, or execution. This is the single most persistent communication risk across the series.
- **The paid-sharing conversion funnel was never reconciled.** Netflix publicly estimated ~100 million borrowing households including ~30 million in UCAN. This estimate was never updated with actual conversion figures across any of the nine quarters despite being the primary 2023 revenue driver. The question was asked directly in Q1 2024 and received a process-substitution answer.
- **Warner Bros. integration opacity.** The $9.7 billion acquisition announced Q4 2025 was characterized as having zero regulatory risk and received no discussion of integration complexity, assumed synergies, cost structures, or underperformance scenarios. For a transaction this size, that is a material disclosure gap.
- **"Perishable goal" pattern in gaming.** The gaming investment thesis has been framed identically across seven consecutive quarters as "early days, scaling with demonstrated returns." No specific proof-point threshold has ever been defined. Without an anchor, the category can remain "promising" indefinitely.
- **Strategic boundary language is softening.** The prohibition on full-season sports rights shifted from categorical (Q4 2023: "I would not look at this as a signal of any change to our sports strategy") to conditional (Q4 2024: "if there was a path where we could make the economics work... we certainly would explore"). Investors should track phrasing in Q1 2026.

## Verify Next Quarter

Based on Q4 2025 tracking commitments and unresolved prior flags:

1. **Advertising revenue toward "$3 billion" in 2026.** Peters committed to "roughly doubling... to about $3 billion." Q1 2026 should disclose whether the trajectory is on pace, with a partial-year run rate. If only directional language appears without a figure, flag as regression from Q4 2025 specificity.

2. **Operating margin of "31.5%" full-year 2026.** Verify whether M&A integration costs are reclassified or excluded in any way that makes the comparison to prior-year margins unclear. Neumann's half-point drag disclosure sets the baseline.

3. **Warner Bros. regulatory approval — conditions and timeline.** Sarandos expressed unqualified confidence. Any conditional approval, behavioral remedy, or extended timeline should be evaluated against that unqualified language. Flag if management revises the framing without acknowledging the prior certainty claim.

4. **"Primary quality metric" disclosure.** Referenced in Q4 2025 as "at an all-time high" but not disclosed publicly. If Q1 2026 continues to cite this metric without definition or investor-accessible benchmarking, escalate as a structural accountability gap.

5. **Cloud gaming TV expansion.** Peters stated roughly one-third of members have access to TV-based games, with plans to expand in 2026. Q1 2026 should confirm whether the expansion occurred and whether engagement data (described as "significant uptick" after the party pack launch) is directionally maintained or declining.

6. **Sports rights boundary language.** Capture the exact phrasing of any sports rights question and compare to Q4 2024's "if there was a path... we certainly would explore." Any further softening is a meaningful strategic signal regardless of whether a deal is announced.

7. **Animation slate accountability.** Sarandos named "In Your Dreams" for Q4 2025. Confirm whether it launched on schedule. The "promising slate through 2027" should receive incremental specificity — Q1 2026 is the first quarter where this can be tracked against named titles.
