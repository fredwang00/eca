**Direction: Oscillating, with a Q4 2025 inflection upward.** The series has never left a narrow B-band across eleven quarters—no quarter scored above B+ or below B-. Q3 2023 (3.10) was the prior high-water mark, driven by unusually strong Q&A specificity from Khosrowshahi. The trough was Q4 2024/Q1 2025 (both 2.65), where FOG density and capital allocation vagueness combined with thin stakeholder disclosure. Q4 2025 (3.135) represents the clearest inflection, attributable to Balaji Krishnamurthy's quantitative density in his first public appearance, stronger AV specificity, and a genuinely structured delivery growth framework from Khosrowshahi. Whether this marks a durable improvement or a one-quarter outlier depends on whether Balaji maintains Prashanth's disclosure discipline.

The structural pattern across all quarters: CFO contributions consistently outscore CEO contributions on the FOG and Capital Stewardship dimensions. Khosrowshahi's prepared remarks are reliably the weakest section of every call. Q&A consistently outperforms prepared remarks.

## Key Commitments

### Signed
- **Waymo partnership** — Named counterparty. Austin and Atlanta deployments disclosed; Austin fleet described as "busier than 99% of Austin drivers." Q4 2025: "30% higher trips per vehicle per day" on Uber platform vs. standalone cited, sourced to "publicly available data" without citation.
- **Waabi exclusivity** — Q4 2025: "First 25,000 passenger vehicles will be exclusive on Uber." Specific vehicle count and exclusivity term disclosed. No financial terms.
- **Nuro/Lucid commitment** — Q2 2025: Named. Capital commitment size explicitly declined. Claimed to "probably yield better economics" than Waymo model without supporting arithmetic.
- **Foodpanda acquisition** — Q3 2024: "$150 million in EBITDA pretty shortly after we close." Named deal, specific accretion claim, no deal size disclosed.
- **Trendyol Go acquisition** — Q1 2025: Named, Turkey market. No purchase price, no closing timeline, no financial terms disclosed. Described only as intended to "supercharge future growth in Turkey."
- **Massachusetts IC-plus settlement** — Q2 2024: Named AG counterparty. Structure described (healthcare benefits, time definitions, no ballot initiative). Financial impact to Uber never quantified despite direct analyst question.
- **BYD EV partnership** — Q2 2024: Named. Specific target of 100,000 EVs. No financial terms.
- **$20 billion share repurchase authorization** — Q2 2025: Sized at "roughly 12% of market cap." Prior Investor Day commitment of "at least 50% of free cash flow to buybacks" tied to this. Q4 2025: Balaji declined to reaffirm the 50% figure, substituting "steady cadence."

### Aspirational
- **"14 AV partners"** (Q3 2024) / **"five AD partnerships in two months"** (Q1 2025) — No commercial terms, deployment timelines, or minimum commitments disclosed for the non-Waymo/Waabi partners.
- **"15 cities by end of 2026"** for AV deployment — Q4 2025. Named commitment but no city list, partner breakdown, or operational definition (safety driver vs. fully autonomous).
- **OEM partnership announcements** — Q1 2025: "Stay tuned, you'll see more announcements with OEMs in the next couple of years." Soft directional claim, no named counterparties beyond Lucid.
- **AV partnership announcements "in coming weeks and months"** — Language appeared in Q2 2024 and again subsequently without resolution. Classic perishable goal pattern.
- **Uber AI Solutions** — Q3 2025: Described as enormous potential, using "core Uber capability" of task dispatch for AI workloads. No named customers, no revenue, no deployment specifics.
- **Uber Elite product launch** — Q3 2025: "Next quarter or two." No economics, pricing tier, or market rollout plan disclosed.
- **Less-dense market expansion** — Referenced Q3 2024 through Q4 2025 as a "tailwind over the next 2–3 years." Q4 2025 provides the most specific data to date: "nearly 75% of U.S. profits from non-top-20 markets."
- **Platform cross-sell** — "Fewer than 1 in 5 consumers active across both Mobility and Delivery" (Q2 2025 baseline). COO appointment cited as structural enabler. No penetration target or timeline defined.

## FOG Patterns

**Persistent phrases across all eleven quarters:**
- "Very, very" as intensifier — present in every quarter. "Very, very well positioned," "very, very early," "very, very constructive." Functions as emphasis without evidence.
- "Tremendous momentum / runway / opportunity" — Q3 2023, Q4 2023, Q3 2024, Q4 2024. Rotates through subjects but contributes no information.
- "We're quite excited / super excited" — Q2 2023 through Q2 2025. Applied to AV, advertising, sparse markets, Uber One, grocery. When everything is exciting, the phrase carries no signal.
- "Record quarter / all-time high" — asserted without specifying the metric setting the record in Q2 2024, Q2 2025, Q3 2025.
- "Humming," "cooking at," "magic happens" — Khosrowshahi-specific metaphors substituting for mechanism description.
- "[Strategic priority] is a huge, huge opportunity" — Advertising (Q2 2023), AV (multiple quarters), sparse markets (Q3 2024–Q4 2025).

**Prepared remarks consistently worse than Q&A** — present in every quarter evaluated. The divergence is structural: scripted sections optimize for messaging; Q&A is where actual disclosure occurs. Investors reading only press releases receive materially lower quality information.

**Linguistic precision: no net improvement over eleven quarters.** Khosrowshahi's FOG patterns are stable habits, not transitional phases. CFO rotation (Chai → Mahendra-Rajah → Krishnamurthy) has been additive on precision. Prashanth's departure creates the primary forward risk to the FOG score, as his Q&A contributions were consistently the cleanest language in any given call.

## Flags & Risks

**Structural accountability gaps:**
- No quarter in eleven has contained a "What Went Wrong" disclosure. Not one miss, execution gap, or competitive loss is voluntarily named. This is statistically implausible for a company operating in 70+ countries across multiple product categories. Its absence is itself a data point.
- Prior-period guidance reconciliation is selective. The February 2024 three-year framework is revisited (Dimension 2 strength), but individual quarter guidance is rarely checked against actuals in prepared remarks. Positive surprises are disclosed; negative variances are absent.
- "Perishable goal" pattern confirmed: AV partnership announcements "coming in weeks and months" (Q2 2024) were never resolved with named commercial terms. The advertising "$1B by 2024" target migrated from a dated commitment to an undated achievement claim across multiple quarters.

**Capital allocation flags:**
- Free cash flow was not disclosed as a dollar figure on the Q2 2025 call despite being the stated basis for the $20 billion buyback authorization.
- The "50% of FCF to buybacks" Investor Day commitment was active through Q2 2025, then replaced by "steady cadence" in Q4 2025. This is a metric retirement without acknowledgment.
- Autonomous vehicle investment scale is described as strategic priority but sized only as "not noticeable in the P&L" — a disclosure that is deliberately uninformative.

**Stakeholder balance flag:** Driver and courier economics are disclosed instrumentally (aggregate earnings cited as headline metrics) but not from a partner perspective. The California insurance legislation win (reducing uninsured motorist limits from $1M to $60K) was disclosed as a cost savings with no acknowledgment of risk transfer to drivers and injured parties. This pattern — presenting regulatory outcomes in investor-favorable framing only — is a repeatable risk to stakeholder credibility.

**CFO transition risk (active):** Prashanth Mahendra-Rajah's departure February 16, 2026 creates a short window where prior commitments may be implicitly reset. Balaji Krishnamurthy's first call (Q4 2025) scored well on quantitative specificity; it is his first appearance, and the capital return answer was the weakest disclosure in an otherwise strong call.

## Verify Next Quarter

From the Q4 2025 call, these are the live tracking commitments for Q1 2026:

1. **"15 cities by end of 2026" for AV.** Verify whether a city list is disclosed, which partners are operating in which cities, and whether deployments include safety drivers or are fully autonomous. The commitment is specific enough to be falsifiable — any change in language or scope should be explicitly noted.

2. **Capital return cadence under Balaji.** The "50% of FCF to buybacks" figure was not reaffirmed. Q1 2026 is the first full quarter under Balaji as CFO. Monitor actual buyback dollar amount vs. the implied ~$2.4B quarterly pace (50% of $9.8B FCF ÷ 4). If the figure is significantly below this without an explicit policy change statement, flag as an accountability event.

3. **Insurance inflection from cost drag to margin tailwind.** Balaji characterized insurance as "going from deleveraging to leveraging." Q1 2026 should show this in either margin expansion or disclosed fare reductions. The Q4 2024 forward estimate of "high single digits per trip inflation in 2025" should now be checkable against full-year 2025 actuals.

4. **Waabi exclusivity claim.** "First 25,000 passenger vehicles exclusive on Uber" — verify whether this commitment appears in any Waabi announcement, regulatory filing, or Q1 2026 commentary. Absence of any corroboration after one quarter is a mild flag; absence across two quarters is a pattern.

5. **MAPC trajectory.** Exited 2025 at 18% YoY growth. Q1 guidance implied continued acceleration. Verify whether MAPC growth rate is maintained or reverses — this is the core audience-growth metric in the three-year framework.

6. **Cross-platform penetration baseline.** "Fewer than 1 in 5 consumers active across both Mobility and Delivery" was established as the Q2 2025 baseline. With the COO appointment as the stated structural fix, Q1 2026 is the first quarter to check whether this metric moves. If no updated figure is provided, note the omission.
