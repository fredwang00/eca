## Key Commitments

**Signed**

- **Waymo partnership (commercial, multi-city):** Deployments confirmed in San Francisco, Austin, Atlanta. Austin fleet described as "busier than 99% of Austin drivers." "30% higher trips per vehicle per day" vs. 1P standalone cited as disclosed utilization benchmark (Q4 2025). "Waymos in the hundreds" in Austin/Atlanta committed for 2025 (Q3 2024).
- **Waabi exclusivity clause:** "First 25,000 passenger vehicles will be exclusive on Uber" (Q4 2025). Named counterparty, specific vehicle count, exclusivity term disclosed — the clearest signed commercial commitment in the series.
- **Massachusetts IC-plus settlement:** Agreed structure (healthcare benefits, time definitions, no ballot initiative) confirmed Q2 2024. Financial impact never quantified on any call.
- **BYD partnership (EVs):** 100,000 EV target disclosed with named counterparty (Q2 2024).
- **Foodpanda acquisition:** "$150 million in EBITDA pretty shortly after we close" — specific, named synergy claim (Q3 2024).
- **Trendyol Go acquisition:** Announced Q1 2025, no purchase price, closing timeline, or financial terms disclosed on any subsequent call through Q4 2025.

**Aspirational**

- **AV partnership pipeline:** "14 AV partners" (Q3 2024); "five AD partnerships in two months" (Q1 2025); "more announcements in the coming weeks and months" — repeated across Q2 2024, Q3 2024, Q1 2025, Q2 2025 without commercial terms for any non-Waymo partner.
- **"15 cities by end of 2026"** for AV deployments (Q4 2025) — named milestone but no partner breakdown, vehicle count, or regulatory condition attached.
- **Uber AI Solutions / "multiple gigs" platform:** Described as "really exciting" with "enormous potential." No counterparty, no revenue target, no timeline in any quarter.
- **OEM partnership announcements:** "Stay tuned, more OEM announcements in the next couple of years" (Q2 2025) — no named partner beyond Lucid.
- **Cross-platform penetration improvement:** "Can and will go much higher over time" (Q2 2025) — no target, no timeline, no intermediate milestone defined.
- **Less-dense market penetration as "tailwind over next 2–3 years"** (Q3 2024) — no baseline metric established in the quarter it was claimed.

## FOG Patterns

**Recurring phrases across quarters (with first and last appearance):**
- "Very, very [adjective]" — Q2 2023 through Q4 2025; no quarter is free of this construction.
- "Tremendous momentum / tremendous runway" — Q2 2023, Q3 2023, Q4 2023, Q4 2024; retired briefly, re-emerges.
- "Quite excited / super excited" — every quarter; functions as a sentence-ending escape valve when substance runs out.
- "Long, long runway" — Q2 2023, Q3 2024, Q4 2025.
- "Best-of-breed" / "indispensable" — AV-specific FOG, Q2 2023 through Q4 2025; asserted without competitive data.
- "Magic happens in a particular city" — deployed twice in Q4 2024; semantic placeholder for a flywheel effect the CFO explains well elsewhere.
- "Cooking at $X billion" — Q3 2024; obscures whether figures are run rate, TTM, or annualized.
- "In the [N]th inning" — Q2 2025, Q3 2025; baseball metaphor substituting for milestone accountability.

**Linguistic precision over time:** Degraded slightly from Q3 2023 (best Q&A specificity in the series) to Q3 2024–Q1 2025 (highest FOG concentration). Q4 2025 shows improvement driven by Balaji's quantitative discipline, but Khosrowshahi's prepared remarks remain structurally promotional throughout the series. The consistent pattern: Q&A is cleaner than prepared remarks in every quarter; the prepared remarks are optimized for messaging, not disclosure.

**Structural FOG pattern:** Every quarter opens with a superlative self-assessment ("standout quarter," "outstanding quarter," "record quarter," "exceptional path") before presenting evidence. No quarter opens with a problem disclosure.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality flags:**
- Free cash flow withheld or unquantified in Q2 2023, Q3 2023, Q2 2025 — notable gaps precisely in quarters where capital return discussions are prominent.
- Massachusetts settlement cost impact: asked directly in Q2 2024, not quantified in any subsequent quarter.
- Trendyol Go: announced Q1 2025, no financial terms in Q1, Q2, Q3, or Q4 2025.
- AV investment sizing: described as "aggressive" and a "top priority" in every quarter from Q2 2024 onward; never sized in dollar terms. "You're not really gonna notice it in the P&L" (Q4 2024) is the only quantitative framing offered.
- Non-GAAP metric shift from adjusted EBITDA to adjusted operating income (Q3 2025): occurs as AV capital deployment and depreciation increase — timing warrants monitoring.

**Communication-level risks:**
- **Accountability vacuum:** No prior-quarter guidance is explicitly reconciled against actuals in any Q&A session. The February 2024 three-year framework is the only external accountability anchor in the series, and it is invoked impressionistically rather than audited line-by-line in most quarters.
- **Perishable goal pattern:** "More AV announcements coming in weeks/months" appears in Q2 2024, Q3 2024, Q1 2025, and Q2 2025 without resolution into named commercial terms. "Less-dense market tailwind over 2–3 years" (Q3 2024) was introduced without a baseline metric. Cross-platform penetration improvement framed as inevitable without a defined target since Q2 2025.
- **Uniform competitive reassurance:** Across 10 quarters and 70+ operating countries, no market is described as showing meaningful competitive deterioration. This statistical improbability signals potential selective disclosure in competitive Q&A.
- **"What Went Wrong" disclosure: absent in every quarter.** Not a single missed target, execution gap, or strategic bet acknowledged as underperforming across the entire series. This is structurally implausible for a company of Uber's complexity and is the single most consistent candor gap.
- **CFO transition risk (active):** Prashanth departed Feb. 16, 2026. Prior commitments (50% FCF to buybacks, insurance cost trajectory) now attributable to a departed CFO; Balaji has not yet formally reaffirmed specific quantitative targets.

## Verify Next Quarter

From the Q4 2025 call, the following should be checked when Q1 2026 drops:

1. **"15 cities by end of 2026" for AV deployments** — Khosrowshahi's most specific AV commitment. Verify city count, named partners, and whether launches involve safety drivers or fully driverless operation.

2. **Capital return cadence under Balaji** — The prior "50% of free cash flow to buybacks" commitment was stated by Prashanth but not reaffirmed quantitatively by Balaji. Verify actual Q1 2026 buyback dollar amount and whether Balaji introduces a new quantitative framework or allows "steady cadence" to remain the operative language. ~$2.45B/quarter implied by 50% of ~$9.8B annual FCF is the falsifiable benchmark.

3. **Insurance inflection to leveraging** — Balaji stated insurance is "going from a deleveraging cost item to something that gives us leverage." Verify whether Q1 2026 unit economics reflect margin expansion or consumer price reduction attributable to insurance, and in which geographies.

4. **MAPC trajectory** — Ended 2025 at 18% YoY growth. Does momentum sustain, decelerate, or accelerate in Q1 2026?

5. **Waabi exclusivity claim** — "First 25,000 passenger vehicles exclusive on Uber." Track any Waabi public announcements for consistency with this claim. Any Waabi disclosure of non-Uber deployment would require explanation.

6. **Trendyol Go** — Now four quarters since announcement with no financial terms disclosed. Q1 2026 is the natural deadline for deal close confirmation, purchase price, and integration timeline. Continued non-disclosure would represent a persistent accountability gap.
