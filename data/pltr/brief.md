## FOG Patterns

The CEO/CFO linguistic split is the defining structural pattern across all nine quarters. Glazer's segments consistently score B to A−; Karp's consistently score C to D. This gap has widened as financial performance has improved.

**Recurring phrases across five or more quarters:**
- "Unprecedented demand" / "never seen in 20 years" / "truly anomalous" — present Q1 2023 through Q4 2025 without quantitative anchoring
- "Uniquely positioned" / "unique position" — appears in every quarter from multiple speakers; never supported with structural evidence
- "Quantified exceptionalism" — introduced Q3 2024, repeats through Q4 2025; defined nowhere, measurable by nothing
- "AI haves and have-nots" — introduced Q3 2024, present in every subsequent quarter; a slogan, not an analysis
- "Warrior culture" / "mission-driven culture" — present Q1 2023 through Q4 2025; assertion, not evidence
- "Most valuable enterprise software company" / "defining enterprise software company of our generation" — introduced Q2 2023, continues without financial scaffolding
- Competitor dismissiveness — "people who couldn't spell GPU," "fake competitors," "products that don't work," "they're crying" — escalating in intensity; Q4 2024 and Q3–Q4 2025 show the most concentrated usage

**Linguistic precision trend: Degrading.** Early calls (Q1–Q2 2023) contained substantive sections from Karp that mixed promotion with genuine investor education (the Sankar ontology explanation, the honest international framing). By Q3–Q4 2025, Karp's prepared remarks and Q&A have become primarily performative — more contempt for critics, less falsifiable content. The FOG Index scored C in Q1 2023 and Q2 2023, C in Q3–Q4 2023, C in Q1–Q2 2024, deteriorating to D in Q4 2024 and D again in Q3–Q4 2025.

**Q&A evasion is a stable pattern.** Direct analyst questions about operational constraints (Q1 2024), international strategy (Q4 2023 through Q4 2025), government budget share (Q1 2025), and sales force scalability (Q2 2025) consistently receive answers that expand into geopolitical or cultural commentary rather than addressing the specific operational question. Sankar is the consistent exception — his Q&A answers are measurably more responsive than Karp's.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality:**
- Q1 2024 revenue figure not extracted from structured data (21% growth referenced, absolute not captured in excerpts)
- Q4 2025 total revenue not disclosed in structured data (segment data available; US commercial $507M)
- Adjusted FCF for Q1 2024 and Q2 2024 not confirmed in structured metrics
- TCV bookings are reported quarterly but weighted-average contract duration is never disclosed; enforceability in government contracts (termination-for-convenience clauses) is never addressed
- Adjusted vs. GAAP gap is widening in absolute terms: Q3 2025 shows $172M SBC quarterly ($688M annualized) producing a 51% adjusted vs. 33% GAAP operating margin — a $208M gap that is noted but not discussed qualitatively on the call

**Communication risks:**
- **The contempt-for-skepticism pattern is the highest-risk communication behavior.** CEOs who frame analytical scrutiny as enemy activity eliminate the social cost of being wrong — which eliminates a useful early-warning signal. This pattern is present from Q1 2023 but intensifies materially from Q4 2024 onward.
- **International commercial is structurally unaddressed.** This segment — representing 27% of revenue — grew 8% in Q4 2025 against 137% in US commercial. Across nine quarters, management has never provided a strategic response: no plan, no milestone, no acknowledgment of execution failure. The explanation has consistently been European cultural or political resistance. This attribution bias is itself a risk signal.
- **Warp Speed remains a perishable goal.** Introduced Q2 2024, described with escalating enthusiasm through Q4 2025, with zero revenue attribution, zero named customers, and zero timeline commitment across seven consecutive quarters. If it disappears from Q1 2026 without explanation, it will have functioned as a narrative vehicle, not a business commitment.
- **The "vindication" rhetorical structure creates no contingency.** The call architecture — "we were right, they were wrong, enjoy" — provides no accountability mode for a disappointing quarter. When results soften, the surrounding rhetoric will have conditioned investors to view disappointment as a temporary aberration rather than a signal.
- **$6B+ cash position has no articulated deployment thesis.** Nine quarters of accumulation; no acquisition framework, no expanded buyback rationale, no R&D deployment commitment. The CFO mentions the buyback balance; no one explains the capital allocation philosophy.
