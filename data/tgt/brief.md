# Target Corporation (TGT) — Earnings Call Communication Brief
### Q1 FY2023 through Q4 FY2025 (9 quarters)

---

## Candor Trajectory

**Grade progression:** C+ (2.4) → C+ (2.4) → C+ (2.3) → C (2.0) → B (2.55) → C+ (2.55) → C+ (2.55) → C+ (2.4) → C+ (2.575)

The trajectory is effectively flat across nine quarters, oscillating between C+ and B without a sustained directional shift. Two inflection points are worth naming:

**Q1 FY2024 (May 2024) — Low point.** The C (2.0) was driven by a call that offered almost no concrete financial data in prepared remarks, declined to quantify a material price investment program announced the same week, and used a relay-answer architecture in Q&A that systematically diffused accountability. This is the only quarter that fell below C+.

**Q2 FY2024 (August 2024) — Brief peak.** The single B (2.55) was earned by Fiddelke's specifically decomposed shrink disclosure (Q2/Q3/Q4 magnitude guided separately), sortation center metrics with trackable unit economics, and honest acknowledgment of a wide comp guidance range. The grade did not hold: Q3 FY2024 returned to C+ despite identical arithmetic because the EPS guidance was raised then lowered with no reconciliation, and unquantified cost surprises resisted two direct analyst questions.

**Q4 FY2025 (March 2026 FCM) — Current.** C+ (2.575) reflects genuine improvement in category-level strategic specificity from new Chief Merchant Cara Sylvester and CFO Jim Lee's funded capital allocation bridge, but is held at C+ by CEO Fiddelke's persistent brand-mythology language and the absence of any systematic revisitation of prior commitments.

**Direction:** Stagnating. The company communicates professionally but has not structurally improved candor quality across the observation window.

## Key Commitments

**Signed:**
- No named contracts with disclosed counterparties, binding dollar values, or specific terms appear in any transcript reviewed. All commercial relationships cited (Ulta Beauty, Marks & Spencer, Disney, Marriott Bonvoy) are described as existing partnerships or announced intentions without disclosed financial terms.

**Aspirational:**
- "$15 billion in incremental revenue over the next five years" — cited at Q4 FY2023 FCM and Q4 FY2025 FCM, never decomposed by year, category, or initiative. Surfaced in Q2 FY2025 only because an analyst raised it; management did not proactively update it.
- "$2–3 billion in efficiency savings" over time — introduced at Q4 FY2023 FCM, with $500 million described as "a down payment." No annual cadence, no timeline, no specific lever breakdown in any subsequent quarter.
- Target Circle 360 membership to "more than triple" over three years — Q4 FY2023 FCM; no membership baseline disclosed against which to track.
- Target Plus GMV to $5 billion by 2030 — Q1 FY2025; current GMV figure not disclosed in same call.
- 6% EBIT margin target — introduced at prior Investor Day, directly questioned at Q4 FY2023 FCM (Gutman: "There was a 6% margin target—we didn't hear about that today"), quietly sidestepped by Lee without confirmation, denial, or explanation. Not revisited in subsequent quarters. Perishable goal, confirmed retired.
- China sourcing below 25% by end of 2026 — Q1 FY2025 (Gomez); prior baseline: 60% in 2017, approximately 30% at time of call. Trackable.
- CapEx of approximately $5 billion for FY2026, 30+ new stores, 130+ full remodels — Q4 FY2025 FCM.
- Q1 FY2026 adjusted EPS "flat to up slightly" from $1.30 — Q4 FY2025 FCM; unusual quarterly-level precision for a company that abandoned quarterly guidance.
- 75% of decorative accessories overhauled by June 2026; 200 Threshold shop-in-shops by summer 2026; Target Beauty Studio in 600 stores by fall 2026 — Q4 FY2025 FCM (Sylvester).

## FOG Patterns

**Recurring phrases across quarters:**
- "Laser-focused" — Q1 FY2023, Q1 FY2024, Q2 FY2025, Q3 FY2025. Not retired despite acknowledged underperformance.
- "More to come" / "we'll unpack that at [future event]" — Q3 FY2023 (CapEx composition, operating margin); Q4 FY2023 FCM (shrink, Circle 360 economics); Q3 FY2025 (accountability timeline, next-year comp framework). A persistent deferral mechanism spanning three years.
- "Play the long game" / "playing our own game" — Q3 FY2024, Q3 FY2025, Q4 FY2025. Deployed as a response to analyst pressure; conveys posture without content.
- "Green shoots" — Q3 FY2024; four instances in a single call without specifying which categories, what growth rate, or over what timeline.
- "Being Target" — Q3 FY2024; used at least three times in Q&A, including as a direct response to "what does Target need to do differently?"
- "Tarzhay magic" / "delivering everyday discovery and delight" — Q1 FY2025, Q2 FY2025, Q3 FY2025, Q4 FY2025 FCM. Increases in frequency as performance deteriorates.
- "Crystal clear" — Q4 FY2025 FCM; three uses in Q&A as the answer to structural questions about strategy durability.
- "Resilient" (consumers) — Q1 FY2023 through Q3 FY2024; upgraded to "resourceful" in Q3 FY2024 (Fiddelke), which is the one instance where a recurring phrase was deliberately refined with behavioral evidence.

**Direction:** Linguistic precision has degraded, not improved, across the observation window. The Cornell era saw moderate FOG with occasional specific passages (Fiddelke on shrink mechanics, Mulligan on sortation centers). The Fiddelke transition era (Q2 FY2025 onward) introduced new vocabulary ("FUN 101," "merchandising authority," "style and design") but has not yet produced measurably higher specificity in the CEO's own framing language. Sylvester and Lee communicate at a higher linguistic standard than the CEO; that divergence is itself a pattern worth noting.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality flags:**
- Free cash flow never reported as a standalone figure in any quarter reviewed, despite buyback resumption being highlighted as a milestone in Q2 FY2024 and again post-pause in Q3 FY2025.
- Roundel revenue cited as "$1.5 billion of value" at Q4 FY2023 FCM without revenue/margin decomposition; growth rates cited in subsequent quarters (mid-teens) without a denominator.
- Target Plus GMV disclosed as $1 billion at Q4 FY2024 FCM but not updated in subsequent quarters with a current-period figure.
- Guidance raised (Q2 FY2024) then lowered (Q3 FY2024) with no explicit post-mortem reconciliation.
- Full-year adjusted EPS guidance maintained at $7–$9 through Q2 FY2025 despite Q2 described as "reinforcing confidence"; guidance range not narrowed when asked directly.

**Communication-level risks:**
- **The accountability deferral loop.** Multiple substantive questions across multiple years are deferred to the next Financial Community Meeting (Q3 FY2023 → March 2024; Q3 FY2025 → March 2026). The March events have not systematically closed prior deferrals.
- **The perishable goal pattern is confirmed.** The 6% EBIT margin target was introduced, questioned, deflected, and quietly retired without acknowledgment across four quarters.
- **Unquantified cost surprises.** Q3 FY2024 had two analysts ask for dollar/basis-point magnitude of "unique" costs; both received category descriptions instead of numbers. This repeated in Q1 FY2025 with tariff exposure.
- **Belonging/DEI traffic impact.** Q1 FY2025 names the January 2025 belonging announcement reaction as a headwind and immediately moves on. No sizing, no duration estimate, no analysis.
- **"We are clearer now" as structural change.** At Q4 FY2025 FCM, Fiddelke's primary response to "what's structurally different?" is leadership alignment and clarity of strategy — both assertions rather than mechanism descriptions.
- **Q&A verbatim repetition.** Q3 FY2025: Fiddelke gave near-identical responses to the Gutman investment question and the Tarlowe CapEx question, suggesting pre-loaded talking points substituting for genuine engagement.
