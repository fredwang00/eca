## Candor Trajectory

**Grade progression:** Q1 2023: B (2.65) → Q2 2023: B (2.935) → Q3 2023: A- (3.625) → Q4 2023: B (3.0) → Q1 2024: B (2.65) → Q2 2024: B (3.0) → Q3 2024: B (2.65) → Q4 2024: B (3.0) → Q1 2025: B (3.0) → Q2 2025: B (2.65) → Q3 2025: B (3.125) → Q4 2025: B (2.65)

**Direction: Stagnating.** The trajectory peaked sharply in Q3 2023 — driven by Beck's anomaly disclosure, which treated a mission failure as an engineering education seminar and earned the only A-range composite in the series. That quarter's grade was an outlier, not an inflection. Since Q4 2023, the company has oscillated between 2.65 and 3.125, never sustaining improvement. The floor is consistently held by Stakeholder Balance (C, recurring) and FOG Index (C or low-B, recurring). The ceiling is capped by the absence of falsifiable commitments on the constellation strategy, which has remained undisclosed across every quarter evaluated.

**Named inflection points:**
- **Q3 2023 (Peak):** Anomaly disclosure, prior guidance accountability, Neutron pricing discipline signal. Best single-quarter communication in the series.
- **Q4 2023 (Reset):** Grade retreated immediately to B as Neutron schedule confidence outpaced testing evidence and the services vision was explicitly deferred.
- **Q3 2025 (Minor recovery):** Spice's voluntary Neutron budget overrun disclosure ($360M vs. $250–300M original) and specific long-term margin targets by segment lifted the score modestly.

## Flags & Risks

**Data quality flags:**
- Q2 2025 headcount cited as "up 85% from the prior quarter" — internal analysis identifies this as almost certainly an error for 8.5%; uncorrected during the call.
- Q4 2025 non-GAAP gross margin guidance cited as "9% to 41%"— obvious transcription error for "39% to 41%"; uncorrected in prepared remarks.
- Free cash flow attribution to "lumpy cash receipts" repeated as a normalizing explanation across Q3 2023, Q3 2024, Q1 2025, and Q2 2025 without specifying which programs drove timing shifts in each instance.
- Solero/SolAero margin target ("30% non-GAAP within two years of January 2022 close") acknowledged as running "a couple of quarters late" in Q3 2024 and Q1 2025 — two separate belated acknowledgments of the same original miss.

**Communication-level risks, synthesized:**
- **Neutron timeline accountability gap.** The first launch target has migrated: 2024 → mid-2025 → H2 2025 → early 2026 → Q4 2026. Each shift is explained with point-in-time rationale (supplier failures, COVID supply chains, tank rupture) but no systematic post-mortem on forecasting methodology has been offered. The budget overrun (30%+) was voluntarily disclosed in Q3 2025 — a genuine positive — but the timeline slippage receives softer treatment.
- **Constellation vision is load-bearing but unfalsifiable.** The entire long-term valuation argument rests on becoming a constellation operator. Across twelve quarters, no milestone, timeline, target market, capital budget, or customer has been disclosed. This is not strategic caution at this point; it is a structural accountability absence.
- **Strategic theme proliferation.** Q4 2025 introduced five new narratives (silicon solar, data centers, MTO, Golden Dome, Rocket Lab Europe) with no milestones attached to any. Golden Dome has been a prepared-remarks anchor since Q2 2025 with no contracted relationship cited. If these themes do not develop specificity by Q2 2026, the pattern is perishable-goal management.
- **Segment guidance reduction.** Q3 2024 collapsed launch/space-systems breakdown into a single top-line figure. Q3 2025 analysis notes pipeline opacity in Space Systems with "zero specificity — no sizing, probability-weighting, or indication of which opportunities are in late-stage pursuit."
- **Mynaric regulatory status.** Q4 2025 deflected a legitimate investor question with "don't believe everything you read." No timeline, probability, or fallback was offered.
- **OpEx miss normalization.** Q2 2023 GAAP OpEx came in above guidance ($59.8M vs. $55–57M guided) without using the word "miss." This pattern of explaining variance drivers without naming the miss has persisted.
